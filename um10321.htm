10.3.2.1 Paraxial section aberrations                                        Next Previous TOC

 

The first-order properties of the entire lens or a portion of it may be controlled with the input

 

{ A / S / MUL / DIV } SECTION name JSSS JSPS

 

where name is one of the following:

 

FFOCL FRONT BACK FOCL NP1 NP2 PP1 PP2 PPSEP ENP EPP POW

GBR GBD GWL GWR

 

and

 

FFOCL

is the front focal length.

FRONT

is the front focal distance.

BACK

is the paraxial back focal distance (not necessarily the current TH).

FOCL

is the focal length of the lenses in the section.

NP1

is the position of the first nodal point from surface JSSS.

NP2

is the position of the second nodal point from surface JSPS.

PP1

is the position of the first principal plane from surface JSSS.

PP2

is the position of the second principal plane from surface JSPS.

PPSEP

is the separation between the two principal planes.

ENP

is the position of the effective entrance pupil, from surface JSSS, of the section as used.

EPP

is the position of the effective exit pupil, from surface JSPS, of the section as used.

POW

is the power of the section.

 

ENA

is the semi-aperture of the entrance pupil

EXA

is the semi-aperture of the exit pupil

ROD

is the object distance as seen by this section

RID

is the image distance as seen by this section

ROS

is the semi-aperture of the object as seen by this section

RIS

is the semi-aperture of the image as seen by this section

OMAG

is the image-to-object magnification

PMAG

is the pupil magnification

ROAL

is the overall length of the section

GBR … GWL

are Gaussian beam parameters.  (See the previous section.)  To use these parameters, the first surface in the group must be 1.
