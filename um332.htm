3.3.2 Curvature Options                                        Previous Next TOC

 

(These options go into an RLE or CHG file, and may be entered interactively with the dialog SPS.)

 

Many kinds of surface shapes may be described in SYNOPSYS.  In some cases, more than a single descriptor may be specified, producing a compound surface.  The basic shapes available include the following; prisms are described in section 3.9.  Pickups and solves are discussed in section 3.3.2.1.

 

flat

grating

spherical

linear spline

conic section of revolution

cubic spline

Zernike polynomial

biradial conic

biconic

flat polarizer

toric

aspheric toroid

holographic

Fresnel

power-series deformation

unusual surface shapes

non-circular zone surface

 

 

A surface may be declared any of these shapes in an RLE file, but once it has been so declared you cannot assign a different kind of shape to the surface without first removing the shape designation that is already present (unless the present shape is flat or spherical).

 

This procedure allows building of compound shapes in some cases.  If a surface already has a curvature and a conic constant, for example, then entering a new value for the RAD or CV merely changes that quantity – but leaves the previous CC (conic constant) unchanged.  To redefine the surface as spherical (without an additional conic constant) use

 

SN SPH.

 

To remove the current shape designation, leaving the surface flat (and available for a different shape) it can be NULLed with the input

 

 

SN NULL

 

in the CHG file, or with a sentence such as "surface 6 = NULL", entered in AI mode (see chapter 15).  Once nulled, the surface may be assigned any other shape.

 

The rules regarding compound shapes are as follows:

 

1.

A conic constant may be added to a sphere, Zernike, spline, or aspheric toroid.

 

2.

Power-series deformation terms may be added to a flat, sphere, conic section, or aspheric toroid.

 

3.

Any shape except holographic, DOE, grating, or polarizer may be declared a Fresnel surface (and the substrates of HOEs and gratings are allowed).

 

4.

All other shapes are uniquely described by their own mnemonics.
