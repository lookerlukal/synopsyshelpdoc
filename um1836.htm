18.3.6 Ghost Image Analysis with Nonsequential Rays                        Previous TOC

 

The program GHPLOT can show the paths of rays contributing to a ghost image, either with one or two reflections.  But since nonsequential mode is not compatible with that feature, it cannot show ray paths that are not sequential.

 

Here is an example of a telescope where BGI reported a paraxial buried ghost arising from surface 13.  Not all of the rays that enter the system find their way to the ghost image, however, and we suspect that some of them may be trapped via TIR errors inside the lens elements.  To examine this situation, we have to put the system in NOSEQENTIAL mode, and make surface 13 a reflector (to model the ghost properties at that surface) and LOOSE (so the rays are free to go where they want).  Then we trace a few rays over that part of the pupil where they fail to reach the ghost image.  The ray paths, shown below, indeed exhibit TIR errors, and the rays go in a variety of directions.  In reality, they would be absorbed at the edges of the lenses, but SYNOPSYS does not consider that since the edges are not optical surfaces.
