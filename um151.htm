15.1 Natural Language Processing                                Previous Next TOC

 

The SYNOPSYS natural language processor is invoked by entering the command

 

AI

 

when at command level, or by clicking on the button .  To exit from AI (and go back to command-only SYNOPSYS processing) enter

 

INTERACTIVE

 

or click on the button .

 

This program provides a very flexible way to interact with SYNOPSYS, and to perform certain tasks not readily accomplished with the normal command syntax.  Among these tasks are displaying and changing certain lens parameters, defining new commands or character strings, and obtaining plotted parametric curves involving up to three different quantities.  Input to AI consists of English sentences.

 

AI mode is started by the startup MACro, and will therefore normally be turned on.  All of the normal command processing can be accomplished in this mode, and the AI capability is then available any time you want it.  The only potential difficulty with this procedure is in the case of a new user, who makes many input errors.  If an input line does not begin with a valid SYNOPSYS command, Al will assume that it is supposed to be a sentence and will try to parse it.  If it is not a correct sentence either, the program will issue the prompt

 

REENTER 4 CHARACTERS STARTING WITH ____

 

where the supposedly incorrect word is displayed. AI then expects you to correct the misspelled word, at which point it will continue to parse the sentence.  When you are entering a sentence that really is intended for AI, this feature saves the labor of retyping the entire sentence all over again whenever you make a typing error – but if the input was in fact an erroneous SYNOPSYS command, rather than an AI sentence, you have to get out of this AI loop before you can go on.  Press the <esc> key.  You can also press the <Enter> key to skip those four characters and continue on.  (If you have only entered four or fewer characters, this in effect also cancels that line.)  Some new users prefer to turn off AI mode until they become familiar with the command syntax.

 

Many of the example sentences given in this chapter are rather lengthy and might appear at first sight to be a cumbersome way to interact with a computer program.  But in many cases the same thing can be accomplished with a very short sentence, and when it cannot, you are probably dealing with a request that would be beyond the capabilities of any optics program besides SYNOPSYS.  To get such a unique capability, just by typing a sentence, is worth the labor of typing it.

 

Also, you will usually have to enter the more lengthy sentences only once.  By properly utilizing the symbol-substitution feature, discussed in section 15.1.3, you can define your own symbols to equal a complicated sentence – which you then do not have to type again. AI sentences (and symbol definitions) may also appear in MACros, where they may be saved and recalled, providing yet another way to access your favorite input without a great deal of typing.  We recommend that you put your favorite set of symbols in the CUSTOM MACro, so they will never need to be typed again.
