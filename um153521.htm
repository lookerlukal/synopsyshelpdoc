15.3.5.2.1 Lens matching modes                                                Previous Next TOC

 

Each potential lens match is evaluated in four ways: the examples are taken forward and backward, and inserted before and behind each group from the starting lens.  These possibilities are shown below.  "SL" refers to the starting lens, and "EL" to the example.

 

 

When the ten matches have been displayed, you are prompted to select one by number.  Entering a number will cause that match to be constructed from the two lenses involved and displayed on the screen (if SNAP is specified).  A summary of the mechanical errors of that combination is printed, showing which of the first-order goals were met and which not.  You are then prompted to select from:

 

Y

to perform a quick optimization of the new lens

 

<CR>

to skip that match and try the next

 

<esc>

to exit from XSYS with that match

 

L

to display the match list

 

NB

to select a different match.
