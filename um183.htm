18.3 Examples of Nonsequential Raytracing                        Previous Next TOC

 

Several examples of nonsequential systems are shown below.

 

18.3.1 Telescope mirror with inside aperture

 

The plot below shows a cassegrain telescope with a reimaging lens following the primary mirror.  Surface 1 is a dummy surface coincident with the secondary mirror, with a CAI to remove the center of the incoming beam, and is declared LOOSE (but not ILOOSE).  Surface 2, the primary mirror, has a CAI marking the perforation, through which loose rays from surface 1 can pass.  Surface 4, the first side of the reimaging lens, is declared CAPTURE, as is surface 6, the focal plane.  All other surfaces are sequential.

 

 

The PER plot shows that a small zone of rays can go through the opening in the primary mirror and be refracted by the lens, without hitting either mirror.  Rays A through D have passed through this zone.  Rays E and F go straight through the entire system without hitting any surface except the focal plane.
