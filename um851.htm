8.5.1 Spot Diagrams                                         Next Previous TOC

 

Two spot diagram programs are available, in addition to the footprint programs described in section 8.4.  The first, SPT, gives a spot diagram at a single field point and focus position, while the feature TFS can generate a composite picture showing the spot diagrams at up to five defocus positions for three fields of view, on a single picture. SPT may be run from the MGI dialog, and TFS from the dialog MTS. See the next topic for examples.

 

The set of rays is by default a square grid, expanded as necessary in the case of an elliptical or rectangular pupil so as to fill it with rays.  If switch 90 is on, the ray grid will be hexapolar instead of square, which is often better for viewing spot diagrams.
