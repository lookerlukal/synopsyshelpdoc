Curvature Solve Dialog

 

Lens surfaces that are spheroidal or conic sections of revolution may be controlled by a curvature solve.  This option automatically calculates the axial radius of curvature that will satisfy a goal expressed in paraxial terms.  The radio buttons on the dialog list the available options, which will control either the height on the next surface or the ray angle after refraction at this surface, as well as maintain an aplanatic or concentric condition.

 

These options provide an efficient way to maintain, for example, a given F/number following a given surface (with a UMC solve where the target value is 1/(2*FNUM), negative if the upper rim ray is going downwards at that point).  This condition will therefore be maintained continuously during optimization, leading to considerable improvement in efficiency and accuracy since the merit function then need not call out that requirement.

 

To use these options, paraxial raytracing must be enabled (see SYS), and to solve in the X-Z plane, the XPXT option must be in effect as well.

 

This dialog is reached via the SPS dialog.
