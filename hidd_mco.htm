Multicolor Analysis Data Dialog

 

The MMC dialog lets you edit wavelength data, assign spectral weights, and select the primary, long, and short colors.

 

Every lens must have one wavelength declared the primary wavelength.  Paraxial raytracing is performed in this color, and for that reason paraxial solves, if any, are based on the paraxial ray in that color.  If the lens has at least three wavelengths, then two others, designated long and short, can be declared as well.  These are the colors at which third-order chromatic aberration is calculated.

 

These data can also be entered into the RLE file.  They are part of the lens description and are saved with the lens.

 

If you change wavelengths, be sure that all of the indices in the lens are of a form that permits recalculation at the new wavelength.  Suitable forms include the GLM glass model, GTB glass table glasses, and the options GLASS and GDF.
