5.5.1 Analyzing a Ghost Image with PGHOST                Next Previous TOC

 

The GHOST command described in the previous section is useful when you want to identify all of the sources of ghost images in the lens.  All of the ghost analysis routines may be run from the dialog MGH.

 

The PGHOST feature is available for analyzing a single ghost image in detail.

 

The PGHOST option, used for optimization, can also be accessed (as can any of the aberration definitions) with the AI composite feature.  Here is how you might find the ghost image size for the ray that reflects from surface 10, reflects again at surface 3, and then goes to the image surface:

 

COMPOSITE

CD1 PGHOST 10 3

= CD1

 

(This is a self-contained sequence, not requiring the AANT or PANT files.)

 

A further option is to turn on switch 47 before you run this sequence; the program will then show the full path of the paraxial ray involved, from surface 1 until it arrives at the image. Caution: do not turn this switch on if you use this feature in optimization; it will produce hundreds of listings that you certainly do not want to see.
