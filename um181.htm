18.1 Nonsequential surfaces                                        Previous Next TOC

 

There are four characteristics that may be assigned to an individual surface to permit nonsequential raytracing, given by the following entries in an RLE or CHG file:

 

SN LOOSE

SN CAPTURE

SN ILOOSE

SN RECTIFY

SN MBOUNCE

 

The first three will assign a surface the nonsequential flags LOOSE, CAPTURE, ILOOSE, or any combination.  If any surface is declared with one of these mnemonics, the system itself is automatically placed in nonsequential mode. RECTIFY applies only to dummy surfaces.

 

To remove any of these characteristics, use the mnemonics NOLOOSE, NOCAPTURE, INLOOSE, and NRECTIFY.

 

The POP listing lists the nonsequential flags that are on, if any.  Surface flags may also be displayed and edited in the SPS spreadsheet program.

 

The system mnemonics SEQUENTIAL and NOSEQENTIAL (note spelling) may also be used to turn on or off the nonsequential mode (see 3.2).
