12.1.3.3 BTOL Strehl ratio quality descriptor                                Next Previous TOC

 

You may select the Strehl ratio quality descriptor with the following input:

 

TOL STREHL VALUE

TOL STF VALUE VALUE …

DEGRADE STREHL FRACTION

DEGRADE STF NB NB …

 

TOL STREHL

specifies that the Strehl ratio is to be evaluated and maintained within the specified tolerance.  This option calculates the wavefront variance, but utilizes the inverse of the quantity

 

                S.R. = exp( -4π**2*variance)

 

as the quality descriptor.  For this case the worst allowable value is smaller than the initial value, rather than the other way round as is the case with SPOT and WAVE.  A field weighting of .5 would allow the S.R. of that point to degrade by one-half as much as would apply with a weight of 1.

 

CAUTION: the Strehl ratio should be requested only for a system that is extremely well corrected.  The budget calculation section of BTOL relies on the assumption that the change of the quality descriptors may be described fairly well by a Taylor's series with but two terms, and this is not true for an exponential such as the S.R. except over a small range.  If the nominal system has a S.R. below about 0.3, the results of this calculation are not very meaningful and the wavefront variance or MTF should be used instead.  For systems with more than about two waves of peak-to-valley wavefront error, the SPOT tolerancing options, rather than any of the OPD-based methods, are recommended.

 

The Strehl ratio descriptor is recommended only for systems with a uniform, unvignetted, circular aperture.  This means that it should not be relied on with Gaussian object systems, in which the aperture is strongly apodized.

Since this calculation is based on the wavefront variance, it automatically includes the ill effects of lateral color.  Even if the image in each color is perfect and the S.R. in each color is high, the variance calculation takes a common image point for describing the wavefront, and lateral color shows up as wavefront tilt – which increases the variance and reduces the S.R.  Of course, the definition of the S.R. becomes ambiguous in such cases, but it is nonetheless a useful tolerancing criterion.

 

See the comments regarding MTF tolerancing, for a discussion of the automatic derivative-correction employed for cases where the quality degrades excessively when calculating the derivatives.

 

TOL STF

gives a tolerance for the Strehl ratio at each field point.

 

DEGRADE STREHL

specifies the fractional change permitted in the Strehl ratio.  For example, to allow the S.R. to degrade to 70% of its nominal value, one would enter DEGRADE STREHL 0.3.

 

See the section on TOL STREHL for additional information and cautionary notes.

 

DEGRADE STF

gives the allowed degradation to the Strehl ratio at each field point.
