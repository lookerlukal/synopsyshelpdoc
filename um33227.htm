A biradial conic surface differs from the biconic discussed above.  Whereas the biconic has the same radius of curvature in both the X and Y directions but different conic constants, the biradial has different radii in the two directions as well.  It is defined by the equation

 

 

This equation states that the surface is a conic section in both the X-Z and Y-Z planes, with semi-major axis b in both cases, and semi-minor axes a and c in the Y and X directions.  The values of a and c follow the rules for symmetrical conics, discussed in Appendix C.  Input is

 

 SN BRD b a c       (note order of parameters).

 

A related shape is found in the USS 17 surface, in which the radius and conic constant both differ in X and Y.

 

To vary these terms, use RAD, CC, and G 1.  (Use all three, because they are coupled.)
