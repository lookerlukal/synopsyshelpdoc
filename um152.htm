The preceding sections have outlined the capabilities of and the rules for operating the SYNOPSYS artificial intelligence feature.  Since this feature is entirely unique, it is expected that users will have no previous experience with anything comparable, and it may not be obvious to many just what it can do.  This section gives a tutorial explanation of some of the possibilities of this part of SYNOPSYS.

 

The AI feature is the easiest part of SYNOPSYS to use but the hardest to document.  Most programs require a specific input data sequence, and documentation is simple; the manual just describes the sequence.  In the case of AI, one would like to avoid giving specific instructions or examples, for fear of implying that that is the only way, or the best way, to get the desired results.  Every task can be done in many different ways, and any brief explanation will be incomplete.

 

However, SYNOPSYS users must be made aware of the possibilities of the AI program, so this section will present a useful subset of AI capabilities.  I hope that users will experiment with the program and discover other situations where its power and flexibility are exactly what is needed.
