GRIN Index Data

 

In the GRIN dialog you can specify the type of gradient-index material you want, and then fill in the data boxes with the coeffients to the equations listed on the right.  The integration length DT should be chosen to provide the best compromise between accuracy and speed; for lenses specified in units of mm, a value of 0.01 is usually adequate. GRINS are limited to only three wavelengths.

 

To get here: Type SPS, then click on the index flag for that surface, then click the radio button ‘GRIN (gradient index)’.  For more information, see Section 3.3.4.1.
