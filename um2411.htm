2.4.1.1 Relative position                                                        Previous Next TOC

 

The simplest rule is RELATIVE.  According to this rule, the nominal position of each surface is on the Z-axis of the previous surface, with a spacing given by the current thickness of that surface.  The X and Y axes of the two are parallel.  If a tilt or decenter is specified, the surface is tilted or moved by the stated amount relative to its nominal position, and its local coordinate system moves with it.  This rule uses the mnemonics AT, BT, and GT for tilts, and DECENTER for decenters, as explained in section 3.8.1.  It is the easiest rule to use in most cases.

 

Under this rule one or more surfaces can tilt or decenter as a group, and the surface following the group remains where it was; the program automatically calculates the reverse tilt and decenter for that surface, which is called the “undo” surface.  Relative tilts and decenters can be picked up by another surface, and the tilt axis need not be located at the vertex of the surface, making this rule powerful enough for most situations.  You may specify any number of tilted or decentered surfaces that utilize the same undo surface, creating an undo stack.  The only requirement is that the undo surface must undo all of the relative tilts and decenters that are in effect at that point, and that require an undo surface.  Thus, you cannot overlap relative regions, and attempting to do so will produce an error message.

 

The undo surface may itself be RELATIVE, LOCAL or GLOBAL; in the latter two cases, the relative tilts and decenters are treated as permanent, without an undo.
