10.8.1 Initial evaluation                                                        Next Previous TOC

 

During the initial evaluation phase of a SYNOPSYS optimization, all of the rays requested in the AANT file must pass through the system without encountering MCS or TIR errors, or else the run will terminate immediately.  This seldom happens except when setting up an initial design or when making a large mechanical change to a previous design.  In such cases, you can often recover very quickly by clicking on the button .  This implements an automated process, described in Section 10.3.1.2.

 

If this does not work, it is useful to trace rays over only a smaller aperture and field for a few iterations while correcting the image.  The full aperture and field may then be traced with less likelihood of ray failure.  Another technique is to vary the quantities YMP1 and YP0, starting at some small value, and to include the desired values in the aberration list.  The program will then move these quantities to the specified value while adjusting the other variables so that the image quality is maintained.  This method works best at finite object distances since the numbers involved are more manageable.

 

Another method to correct for ray failures involves moving things around in the WorkSheet.  You can watch the ray paths change as you move the sliders, and can often find a setting that lets the rays through to the image.  Then the optimization should work.
