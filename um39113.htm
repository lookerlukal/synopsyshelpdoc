3.9.1.13 Double Dove prism (DDOVE)                                Previous Next TOC

 

When one puts two dove prisms together, one gets the double dove.  This is a complicated affair with some curious behavior.  It is generally used only for scanning a beam in collimated light.

 

This prism cannot accept a user-input index since the dimensions are valid only for the glass BK7.  The first and last surfaces of each of the two prisms are dummy surfaces, making four in all, and the required 45-degree tilts are assigned to the surfaces enclosed by them.  In total, the prism requires 10 surfaces.

 

The strange behavior happens because the upper and lower halves of the input beam are inverted and then stitched together.  So the upper and lower rim rays wind up close together near the center of the output beam, while the rays near the axis wind up at the edges.  For this reason, the default clear aperture calculation has little meaning on surfaces beyond the prism, and you are advised to put your own CAOs on those surfaces.  The DDOVE prism also requires nonsequential mode, which traces more slowly than normal.  And whereas one can turn on switch 37 to force the other nonsequential prisms to trace in sequential mode, that does not work with this prism.  So you will have to let it trace in nonsequential mode.

 

Yet another quirk is the fact that the chief ray, which ought to be at the center of the exit pupil, comes out near the edge.  For this reason, the real-pupil search will fail unless the stop surface occurs before the prism.  All of this means that if you want to use this prism type, it is best to put it at the end of the system rather than near the input.

 

Still another is the fact that the two prisms involved do not maintain phase coherence between the beams that pass through them.  This means that you should not ask for OPD calculations at an image analysis other than the on-axis point.  At steep tilt angles, where most of the beam is contained in only one of the prisms, you can do OPD analyses, but you will need to set the ICR parameter to that part of the pupil that goes through that prism.  And even though you can analyze the OPD for the center of the object, be aware that when the prisms are manufactured, the thickness tolerances will not ensure phase coherence anyway.  As a result, the structure of the diffraction pattern and the resolution of the system will be altered.

 

And there’s one more: this prism comes with a dummy surface at the front end to provide a coordinate system for the other surfaces.  Do not alter this surface or change the assigned thickness, which must be zero.

 

If other indices of refraction are desired, the prism flag should be removed with NPRISM and the individual surfaces changed by hand.

 

Number of faces: 10

Z-axis after prism: reversed

Total glass path: 2.3792*WIDTH (for BK7)

Air path from dummy surfaces: 1.0*WIDTH

 

RLE input:

 

SN PRISM DDOVE WIDTH
