Y-YBAR Diagram Dialog

 

The Y-YBAR diagram is found by clicking on the button while the SketchPad is displayed.

 

The Y-YBAR diagram shows a plot of YA versus YB at each surface in the lens.  YA is the paraxial marginal ray; it starts at the on-axis object and passes through the entrance pupil at full aperture.  YB is the paraxial chief ray; it starts from the object at full field and passes through the center of the entrance pupil.  As these rays encounter each surface, they form a characteristic diagram.

 

These two rays convey information about the first-order properties of the lens.  A stop surface, for example will always have YB = zero, and an image surface is a place where YA = zero.  It is possible to lay out a lens system to first order by drawing in the kind of Y-YBAR diagram you want.

 

To use this feature, first select the surface whose radius or thickness you want to control via the Y-YBAR diagram.  This can be done by clicking on the vertex of that surface in the PAD display, or by entering that surface number in the WorkSheet (if that feature is open) and clicking “Update”.  An edit handle appears in the Y-YBAR diagram on the node for the next surface.  It is the coordinates of the paraxial rays on that surface that you are going to control by clicking and dragging the edit handle.  The current values of the radius and thickness are displayed in the Y-YBAR dialog.  When you have selected a surface, you will see two blue lines running through the selected node.  These show the trajectory of that node were you to change just the radius or just the thickness.  They are distinguished by a dash pattern for the radius and a dot pattern for the thickness.  If the lens contains a paraxial pickup or solve, then one or both of these parameters is not free to move and the corresponding blue line is not drawn.

 

As you click and drag the node, the Y-YBAR pattern is continuously updated along with the PAD display.  To save a checkpoint of the current lens state, click “Checkpoint”, and to undo your changes back to that version, click “Undo”.
