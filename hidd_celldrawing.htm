Lens Cell Drawing Editor

 

Once your lens cell is designed, you must open this dialog to make mechanical drawings of the parts.  This step requires you to select the parts of the cell that you want to convert to a drawing, and then click "Define new".  It then makes two new cell descriptions, one with just the requested parts, and the other with those same parts rendered as vectors, or lines, without solid colors.  It is the latter that you will embellish with fiducials and dimensions.

 

Cell part No.

Here you select which part numbers of the lens cell you wish to convert to a drawing.  Note that there are three separate numbering systems.  The first is the lens cell itself, shown on the Lens Cell Editor.  The second applies to the local cell, which contains only the requested part(s), and the third applies to the individual lines and numbers on the outline drawing.  This number applies to the original cell.

 

Draw whole cell

If you want to show the entire cell on a drawing, select this option.

 

Include parts of same color

Contiguous parts of the same color can be drawn as a single part.  This is how you make parts that are more complex than just a simple cylinder.

 

Show cell part numbers

This toggles the display of the part numbers of the reduced cell, containing only those parts requested above.  The color display option must be on to see these.

 

Dimension lines: piece part

Here you can specify the location of lines to be drawn from a point in the cell, where you will eventually place arrows with dimensions nearby.

 

Double lines

Select this option if you want two lines.  This applies to the OD or ID only, where you want lines on top and bottom.

 

Numbers

This toggles display of the numbers assigned to individual lines and dimension numbers.  The color display must be off to see these.

 

All

Select this option to show the line numbers for all lines, including those in the cell outline.  Otherwise only your added lines and dimensions are displayed with numbers.  Although the vector rendering does a good job of ignoring lines between same-color parts, there are times when an extra line will show up, especially on slanted parts.  With this option (and Numbers) selected, you can identify and delete the offending line.

 

Micro

This makes the slider motion 100 times more sensitive, for precise placement.

 

Color fill

Toggles the display of solid colors or outlines.

 

Red

Selecting this box will cause the line currently being edited to be drawn in red, for easier identification.

 

Edit, Delete this

These function the same as in the Cell Editor.

 

Add line

When you have selected the location and direction of the line you want to add, click here to show that line.

 

Dimensions and arrows:

Here you select the part numbers and locations of two points.  These will define the arrows and dimensions that you can then apply.

 

Add arrows

This adds the requested arrows to the drawing.

 

Add dimensions

This adds a number to the drawing, giving the distance between the requested points.

 

(Arrow selection)

These radio buttons let you select how your next arrows will be oriented.

 

Save

Click here to save the drawing that you have created.  You can later open it and make changes, if you wish.  Be sure, in that case, that the PAD display is first set up exactly the same way, and that the lens cell is opened in the Cell editor first.  You can also save a drawing of the cell, but that cannot be edited except for additional annotation.

 

Open

Opens a cell drawing.

 

Recycle

This feature allows you to recycle the lines and dimensions you created on a drawing, and apply them to a new drawing, perhaps with an altered lens cell.  See Section 13.17 for instructions, which must be followed carefully.

 

Update

This button redraws the display.  It is also useful if a slider reaches the end of its range and you want to go further.

 

Checkpoint

This saves a snapshot of the current drawing.  Use it frequently.  You never know when you will screw up.

 

Load check

And this is what you click when you screw up and want to restore your last checkpoint.

 

Make graphics

When your cell drawing is complete, click here to turn it into a standard SYNOPSYS graphics file.  This can be saved, printed, and annotated.

 

Close

Closes the drawing editor.  Any unsaved drawings will be lost.
