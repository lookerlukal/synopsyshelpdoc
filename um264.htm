2.6.4 CSTOP Option                                                                Previous Next TOC

 

The options described above define the chief ray and in some instances the aperture of the entering beam.  If the WAP 2 option is selected, the stop must have a hard aperture before the beam size can be determined.  During the design phase, however, the stop clear aperture may be varying along with other properties of the lens, and in this case it is not practical to input a fixed value so as to satisfy the WAP requirement.  In this case the system parameter

 

CSTOP [REAL XEN YEN [ MARGIN ] ]

 

may be entered in the RLE or CHG file.  This option traces the paraxial ray at the value of YMP1 entered in the original lens file, and then places a hard CAO on the stop surface at the aperture given by this ray.  With this option, the stop CAO will float during the design, always giving the original paraxial entering beam diameter or F/number, and will provide a reference for all field points subject to a WAP 2 pupil size adjustment.  This option is also explained in the Pupil Wizard dialog.

 

The REAL XEN YEN option sets the CAO of the stop by tracing a real ray at the fractional paraxial pupil coordinates XEN, YEN rather than a paraxial ray.  This sets the aperture to the exact value of the real-ray intercept.  Occasionally one finds that ray grids generated using the WAP 2 or 3 options will trace skew rays that exceed this aperture by a small amount, due to high-order pupil aberrations or round-off errors.  To avoid having those rays declared vignetted, you can enter a small MARGIN, which gives the amount by which a ray intercept at the stop can exceed the aperture and still be considered unvignetted.  A margin of a few microns is usually sufficient, if this parameter is needed at all.

 

For ZFILE zoom lenses, the CSTOP is calculated only in zoom 1 to ensure that the same aperture will apply to all defined zooms.  In the event that you want the calculation to be performed separately at each zoom, be sure to include the directive ZCSTOP in the ZFILE section of the RLE file.  Then each zoom will get its own aperture value.

 

For users with experience with the Zemax™ program, the pupil model that most closely follows the logic in that program is given by the combination of CSTOP and WAP 2.

 

To remove a CSTOP, use NOFILL.
