18.1.1 LOOSE surfaces                                                        Previous Next TOC

 

If a surface is defined LOOSE, then rays leaving that surface are free to travel to any surface that is qualified to intercept them.  The surfaces numerically adjacent (both before and after) are always qualified, even if nonsequential flags have not been set on them; other surfaces are qualified only if they are declared CAPTURE. The final surface cannot be made LOOSE; once a ray arrives there, it is stopped (it can, however, be declared CAPTURE).  When more than one surface is qualified to intercept a loose ray, the program selects the one giving the shortest path length in the direction of propagation.  It does not check to see if there are any non-CAPTURE surfaces along the way.  If other surfaces may intercept the ray, they must be declared CAPTURE, in order for rays to arrive at them.

 

The LOOSE flag may be removed with the entry NOLOOSE.
