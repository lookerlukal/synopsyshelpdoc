5.40 Lens blank cost (GCOST)          Previous Next TOC

 

A utility is provided to help you estimate the cost of lens blanks.  The command is

 

 GCOST [ JSSS JSPS ].

 

The feature is also found in the dialog MUT.

These data list the manufacturer’s cost factors, which indicate the price of each glass type relative to that of the least-expensive glass from that vendor, which is usually equivalent to type BK7.  Note that if any surface uses an obsolete glass or one that does not contain cost data in the glass tables, it will not be considered in the total cost calculation.

 

Disclaimer:  The cost data in the glass tables were accurate when compiled, but manufacturers may change their prices without notice, and the price charged depends on the volume of the order and the quality grade selected.  The results of this analysis should be considered only a rough guide, and you are urged to contact the vendor before making critical decisions affected by cost.

 

There are two kinds of analysis performed.  The first section assumes that lens blanks will be ordered molded to approximately the shape of the finished lens, while the second assumes that you will order flat blanks and generate the curves yourself.  More glass is required in the latter case, and the cost factor is therefore higher.  Both sections assume that you will remove about 5% of the glass in each of three dimensions during generating and grinding.

 

The analysis is performed over the entered surface range (JSSS to JSPS) if entered, and the entire lens if not.

 

The cost of molded blanks takes account of any perforation in the element, while the cost of flat blanks does not.
