Insert Prism Dialog

 

You can open the Insert Prism dialog by clicking on the button when the WorkSheet is open.

 

This dialog lets you select from twelve types of prisms plus a roof mirror.  When you select the prism of your choice, enter the width of the first face in the edit box labeled “WIDTH”.  This is normally twice the clear aperture that is required at the point where you want to put the prism (since the latter is a radius, not a diameter).

 

Then you simply click on the PAD drawing at a point within an airspace close to the optical axis where you want the prism to go.  For best results, this airspace should be large enough to contain the prism.  The program will adjust the airspace following the prism to account for the prism thickness and index.  The prism will initially be assigned the glass type BK7, and the airspace calculation is performed on the index of this glass at a wavelength of .58756uM.  You may have to adjust the airspace if your primary wavelength is different.  The program automatically reverses the thickness of surfaces following the prism if it is a type that requires a sign change afterwards.

 

You should not insert a prism if the next surface is GLOBAL, since the folded optical path requires this surface to assume a different position.

 

To change the prism dimensions after it is inserted, click inside the prism near the first face and then edit the width parameter in the WorkSheet dialog box and click update.  To remove a prism, click on the remove-prism button and then click on the first side of the prism at the axis point.

 

Another option is used for the common situation where you want to model a prism with a slab of glass in the initial design stages.  Optimization can be faster, and the PAD D drawing is then appropriate for monitoring the design.  (Once a real prism is inserted, the perspective drawing is required for rendering the shape properly.)  But when the design is stable, you may want to change the slab into a real prism in order to show the actual system geometry.  If your system currently contains a dummy slab, then in the Insert Prism dialog, select the option “Replace dummy glass slab with prism”.  Then select the prism you wish to substitute for the slab and enter the width, as usual.  When you then click on the slab in the PAD drawing, it is removed and the prism substituted, with the same index as the slab.

 

The reverse procedure is available in the Remove Prism button.
