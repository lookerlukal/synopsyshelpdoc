3.8.5 Group coordinates                                        Previous Next TOC

 

The group tilt and decenter feature is similar to the relative tilt described in Section 3.8.1,  but allows tilts in three axes at the same surface.  The input is as follows:

 

 SN GROUP NSURF

 SN GROUP POSITION XDC YDC ZDC

 SN GROUP ANGLE ALPHA BETA GAMMA .

 

Like the relative feature, this option puts into effect an automatic undo at the surface past the group size declared with NSURF.

 

A decenter of the vertex may be declared with the XDC, YDC, and ZDC parameters, while the tilt angles are given by ALPHA, BETA, and GAMMA.  The directions of these parameters are the same as for relative tilts, as described in that section.

 

The decenter is performed first, in untilted coordinates, and the angles are always implemented in the Euler order alpha, beta, and then gamma.

 

This declaration also affects the parameters toleranced by BTOL.  Any lens or group declared as a group will get tolerances on all six degrees of freedom.  This is intended for systems with free-form surfaces, where the tolerance in X and Y and in the three angles can be quite different and should be investigated separately.

 

You may change from any of the other tilt formats to the group format with the input

 

CHG

SN GROUP

END

 

The decenters may be varied in optimization with the PANT mnemonics

 

VY SN XDC

VY SN YDC

VY SN ZDC

 

which are the same as those used for relative decenters, except that here you do not need to give the group size with the variable input, as the relative format requires.

 

Tilt angles can be varied with the mnemonics

 

VY SN GPA                ! group alpha

VY SN GPB                ! beta

VY SN GPG                ! gamma

 

 

You can also give targets to the decenter in the AANT file using the same mnemonics, for example,

 

M 22.5 1 A YDC 14,

 

which gives a target of 22.5 to the Y-decenter of surface 14.
