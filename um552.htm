5.5.2 Analyzing a Ghost Image with RGHOST                Next Previous TOC

 

To evaluate the path of a selected real ray in a specified ghost, the command is

 

 RGHOST JREFH JREFL XEN YEN [ SURF ].

 

where JREFH and JREFL are the high and low surface numbers involved, XEN and YEN are the fractional pupil positions of the (axial) ray to be traced, and the optional SURF gives a surface-by-surface path of the ray.

 

Thus, to see the path of the ray at the edge of the pupil that reflects from surface 12, then from surface 4, and then goes to the image, one would enter

 

RGHOST 12 4 0 1 SURF.

 

This feature traces the selected ray exactly, with all tilts and decenters accounted for.  It does not check for aperture violations.  Usually it is sufficient to observe that the ghost is far enough out of focus, or completely out of the field, so that its intensity is negligible.

 

You can also evaluate real-ray ghosts with GHPLOT.  That feature takes apertures and vignetting into account, and gives plotted output.

 

All of the ghost analysis routines may be run from the dialog MGH.
