13.3.2 The WorkSheet program                                                Previous Next TOC

 

The toolbar button in PAD sends you to the WorkSheet program.  This feature is intended to give you instant feedback as you alter the lens in a variety of ways.  Toolbar buttons are shown at the top of the PAD display (in WorkSheet mode) with which you can insert and remove surfaces, fold mirrors, and elements, flip an element or mirror around, or create a checkpoint to which you can revert with the Undo button.  Another button brings up the stock lens display, where you can match an element to a stock lens and put it into your system.  You can also open PAD and WorkSheet simultaneously with the command WS.

 

The Worksheet also shows an edit window that displays the parameters of a selected surface in RLE format, which you may edit.  When you click on the Update button, the changes are applied to the lens and the PAD display is updated.  You may enter anything in this window that you can put in a CHG file, and you are not restricted to the currently selected surface.

 

In addition, four slider bars are provided with which you may alter any parameter in the RLE file, including the curvature, bending, and thickness of a surface, or slide an element along the axis – all the while monitoring the effects with the PAD display.  You can even select a data item in the edit window – not otherwise assigned to a slider – and vary it with the top slider after clicking on the SEL button.

 

A discription of these features is given at more length in the link to WorkSheet.
