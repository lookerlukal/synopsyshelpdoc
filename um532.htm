5.32 F/number calculation                                Previous Next TOC

 

Among the quantities calculated and printed by the paraxial raytrace PXT is the system F/number, but the value displayed applies only at the center of the field and is based on paraxial rays rather than real rays.  Another feature is available to find the F/number in both X and Y at a given field point, using real rays instead of paraxial rays.  The angle PHI between the upper and lower rim rays is calculated, and the effective F/number is then given by

 

FNUM = 0.5/SIN(0.5*PHI).

 

The program finds the angle in the X-direction by tracing the skew rays to the right and left of the chief ray.  Input is

 

FN HBAR GBAR

 

HBAR and GBAR have their usual meaning; fractional field points in Y and X.  A sample output is shown below.  The calculation is performed at all wavelengths that have been defined in the lens.

 

SYNOPSYS AI>FN 1 0

F/NUMBER AT REL. FIELD (HBAR,GBAR)  1.0000 0.0000

WAVELENGTH    TANGENTIAL     SAGITTAL

 0.656270     11.694085     11.667210

 0.587560     11.732389     11.708724

 0.486130     11.798257     11.782950

 

 

A second form will plot the effective F/numbers at 21 points over the field in the major color.  Input is

 

 FN PLOT

 

For example, here is the plot for a lens with cos**4 darkening (plus some distortion).  The F/number increases near the edge of the field.
