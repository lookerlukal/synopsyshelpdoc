3.3.3 Thickness Options                                Previous Next TOC

 

(These options go into an RLE or CHG file, and may be entered interactively with the dialog SPS.)

 

Lens thicknesses – more properly axial separations, since airspaces are also treated – are entered by one of the following options:

 

SN TH NB

 

SN PTH NB [ M [ B ] ]

pickup thickness of surface NB.  Minus sign changes sign of result. M and B are used to scale and offset the result:

 

TH = TH(NB)*M + B

 

SN YMT NB

thickness solve giving specified paraxial axial marginal ray coordinate on next surface

 

SN YPT NB

thickness solve giving specified paraxial principal ray coordinate on next surface

 

SN NTOP

removes any thickness pickup or solve, retaining the latest value.

SN XMT, XPT

are the same as YMT and YPT, but in the X-Z plane.  The default thickness is zero for all surfaces.

 

A thickness entered with any of these options has no effect if the next surface has been declared GLOBAL or LOCAL.
