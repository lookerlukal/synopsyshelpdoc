5.45 Fresnel Transmission Loss (FTRANS)                Next Previous TOC

 

A rigorous analysis of the transmission of a light ray may be carried out by PRAY, once all of the surfaces have been assigned the appropriate coatings and the system put in polarization mode.  That analysis will treat all surfaces, including buried surfaces (with glass on both sides).  If a cement layer has been explicitly modeled as a thin layer of the appropriate index of refraction, then reflection losses at the cement are also evaluated, in that mode.

 

The FTRANS feature, described in this section, provides a way to get a quick estimate of the losses that occur at cement layers and does not require assignment of coatings or activating polarization mode.  It analyzes the reflection at a buried interface according to the Fresnel equation

 

 REFL = (N’ – N)**2/(N’ + N)**2,

 

which is accurate at normal incidence.  There are two modes, selected by the command

 

 FTRANS { CONTACT / CEMENT [ INDEX ] }.

 

If the CONTACT option is selected, the program assumes that the two glass types on either side of a buried surface are in perfect contact and there is only a single reflection to account for in each case.  If the CEMENT option is selected, the program will model two reflections at each buried surface coming from the two sides of a presumed thin layer of cement, whose index of refraction is given by INDEX, with a default value of 1.55.  These options are for cases where the two glass types are declared in the lens file as adjacent materials; i.e. you did not model the thin cement layer as an extra surface with the index of the cement.

 

But one sometimes does model a cement layer with two explicit surfaces with a small separation between them, and for some fussy systems this degree of attention to detail is called for.  In that case, one would select the CONTACT option, since both sides of the cement layer are declared in the lens and they are in contact with the glass on either side.  In the more common case where one describes a cemented interface with just a pair of adjacent glass types, the cement layer itself is implied but not actually modeled in the lens file.  In this case one could select the CEMENT option so the program will pretend that the extra layer is present and will model it.  No other sources of loss are considered by FTRANS other than the Fresnel losses described.

 

The FTRANS analysis may be found in the dialog MTR.
