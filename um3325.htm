3.3.2.5 Gratings                                                Previous Next TOC

 

(These options go into an RLE or CHG file, and may be entered interactively with the dialog SPS.)

 

The grating described in this section is modeled as a HOE, requiring two surfaces; a simpler form of plane grating is type USS 27, which takes only a single surface.

 

A grating may be considered a special case of a transmission HOE, where both illuminating points are at infinity and the fringes are parallel and of constant separation.  This kind of surface may be input with the input

 

SN GRATING { X / Y } L/MM ORDER CWAV HIN VNUM

 

where the entry X indicates that diffraction takes place in the X-Z plane (fringes parallel to theY axis) and Y indicates diffraction in the Y-Z plane.  The grating frequency in lines/mm is given, as well as the order.  The rules for entering a grating are exactly the same as those for HOEs, described in the previous section, except that only a single line of input is required instead of the several lines necessary for the latter.  Briefly summarized, these rules are:

 

1.

Two surfaces are required for a grating.  The first is a dummy given no index difference, curvature or thickness.  This surface is assigned the GRATING input.  The second of the two surfaces defines the shape, CAO, and the index of the substrate.  If reflective, the second surface must be so declared.  A coating may be assigned to the second surface.

 

Tilts, decenters, and global or local coordinates are assigned to the first of the two surfaces.

 

2.

The program will calculate the blaze angle that gives 100% efficiency at the wavelength CWAV, and will calculate the diffraction efficiency at any other wavelength according to the values of  the index of the emulsion as given by HIN,, and the dispersion as given by the Abbe number VNUM., calculated at the actual wavelengths in the lens, not necessarily the C, d, F lines.  The result will be calculated by the PRAY command.

 

3.

A grating order of -1 will diffract light in the +Y or +X direction.

 

4.

To remove a grating, do SN NDEF; and then enter the new specifications.

 

Example grating:

 

RLE

ID GRATING_REAL FLAT

WAVL .6000000 .5000000 .4000000

APS               1

POLARIZATION LINEAR Y

UNITS MM

OBB  0.000000   10.00000    1.00000    0.00000    0.00000    0.00000    1.00000

  0 AIR

  1 GRA Y        500.0000   -1       0.500000      1.500000     50.000000

  2 COATING PERF

  2 CV      0.0000000000000   TH  1.00000000E-06

  2 N1 1.51628919 N2 1.52140818 N3 1.53084089

  2 CTE   0.710000E-05

  2 GTB S    'BK7             '

  3 COATING PERF

  3 AIR
