MOM – Merit Function Definition: Single Rays

 

This dialog lets you add individual rays to the merit function.  Often you will want to control not just the intercept of a ray, but the difference between that of two rays.  Here is a case where the MACro input for optimization is actually easier to set up than the dialog input.  To correct chromatic aberration, for example, you may want the rays in colors 1 and 3 to coincide at the 0.7 zone of the lens.  The command input (that goes into your AANT file)would be

 

M 0 weight

A 1 YA 0 0 .7

S 3 YA 0 0 .7

 

To be sure, you have to know the formats – but the parameters are very similar to what is shown on the dialog.  See section 10.3.1.2 of the User’s Manual for details.

 

To use the dialog, click on a ‘USE’ box, and then enter the parameters you want.  You can type the pupil and field points yourself, or just click on the ‘Field Point’ and ‘Point in Pupil’ diagram at the appropriate location.
