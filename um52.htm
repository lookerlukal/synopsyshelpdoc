5.2 Element Weight Calculation (WGT, GWGT)        Next Previous TOC

 

There are two forms of weight calculation: WGT and GWGT.

 

The command

 

WGT SN [ SPG ]

 

will calculate the volume and weight of the lens element bounded by surfaces SN and SN +1.  The lens edge will be a cylinder whose radius equals the larger of the CAO values on the two sides, or the edge defined by the current EFILE data if switch 39 is on and EFILE data have been defined. SPG is the specific gravity of the lens, in grams/cc.  Although aspheric surfaces may be analyzed, the results make sense only if the surface is rotationally symmetric.  This feature may also be run interactively with the MUT dialog.

 

WGT can also analyze the weight of a block of glass with flat surfaces and rectangular (RAO) edges on both sides that match exactly.  This is used for beamsplitter cubes.

 

If the glass is a GLM glass or from the glass tables, the specific gravity will be estimated or taken from the glass attributes file, and need not be entered in the data.

 

If EFILE data are in effect and switch 39 is on, the weight calculation takes into account the geometry of the edge, including any flat, angle, or bevel that has been defined.

 

The program can calculate the weight of a mirror, provided the mirror thickness has been entered with EFILE data, or with the MEW edge wizard, and switch 39 is on.  In this case you must enter the specific gravity of the mirror material with the input, using the GWGT form (see below).

 

If the lens or mirror has a CAI in effect, the program will approximate the reduction in weight from removing the circular or rectangular core.  It does not treat elliptical apertures defined with either EAO and EAI, but rectangular apertures are treated.

 

This calculation ignores any tilt or decenter on side 2 of the element, as well as any decenter of the CAOs on either side.  Thus, it takes the current aperture as if were centered at the axis when calculating the element volume.

 

The weight of an element may be controlled during optimization with the parameter WGT, with simpler assumptions.

 

Be aware that if your system contains a Mangin mirror, where the light traverses twice, the weight will be counted both ways, since, to the program, it looks like another element.

The GWGT command has two forms. The first form causes the weight of an included range of surfaces to be computed.

 

GWGT [ JSSS JSPS ] [ P ]

END

 

The second form

 

GWGT [ INCLUDE / EXCLUDE ] [ P ]

SN [ SPG ]

…

END

 

will calculate the weight of the surfaces specified in the surface list following the command. SN in each case should be the first side of the element you wish to evaluate.

 

If the INCLUDE option is selected, only the entered surfaces will be included in the group; the EXCLUDE option will include the whole lens except for the excluded surfaces.  The optional P gives a listing of the individual element calculations as well as the group total.

 

To evaluate mirrors that occur in the surface range, you must use the INCLUDE form, where you can enter the SPG of the mirror material.
