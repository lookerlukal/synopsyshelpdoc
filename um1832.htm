18.3.2 Segmented aperture system                                        Previous Next TOC

 

In the RLE file given below, surface 1 receives the entering beam of light from the object and sends it either to surface 2 or surface 4, both of which are CAPTURE surfaces with rectangular apertures defining one side of a segmented window.  The two windows are given slightly different thicknesses, causing one half of the beam to be retarded in phase relative to the other half.  Surfaces 1, 3, and 5 are LOOSE, and surface 6 is CAPTURE.  Note that PXT's have been defeated (with NPXT); paraxial raytracing is always sequential, and it is not meaningful to send a beam through both windows at the same time.  The system is shown below, followed by a PUPIL plot. Notice that the wavefront has a stepwise discontinuity in the center of the aperture.
