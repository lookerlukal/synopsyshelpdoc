3.9.1.9 Roof mirror (MROOF)                        Previous Next TOC

 

Number of faces: 4

Z-axis after prism: normal

Total air path: 2.0*WIDTH

 

RLE input:

 

SN PRISM MROOF WIDTH

 

Although the roof mirror is not really a prism, it uses the capability of the prism library to generate the decentered apertures and nonsequential flags required to draw and raytrace the roof properly.  This feature places the two roof surfaces at a distance of one WIDTH from the entered SN, which will be a dummy surface and should be assigned AIR or MAIR.  A final surface, also in AIR, follows the roof at a distance of one WIDTH, with the correct global angles to follow the emerging optical axis.  The roof is assigned apertures sufficient to pass a circular beam of diameter WIDTH aligned with and parallel to the optical axis.  The beam is reflected by 90 degrees in the Y-direction.
