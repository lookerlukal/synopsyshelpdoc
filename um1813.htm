18.1.3 ILOOSE surfaces                                                        Previous Next TOC

 

If a surface is declared ILOOSE, sequential rays that intercept it within the inside clear aperture (CAI, EAI, RAI, or UAP type 4) will pass through unchanged and become LOOSE rays.  (If it is not ILOOSE, such rays are vignetted if VIG mode is on.)  Rays hitting the active area do not become loose unless the surface also has the LOOSE flag set.  Loose rays can always pass through an inside aperture of a CAPTURE surface, even if ILOOSE is not set on that surface.

 

An exception is made in the case of a chief ray to be used for image evaluation or correction: to accommodate the frequent case where the system has a central obscuration but the (vignetted) chief ray is still useful as an image position reference, the ILOOSE option will be bypassed when a chief ray is traced, causing the ray to be traced sequentially to the next surface regardless of whether it falls inside the CAI.

 

An ILOOSE flag may be removed with INLOOSE.
