Options|Graphics|Set Device Scale and Offset

Options|Set Device Offset, Margins, Font

 

This menu item calls up a dialog where you can specify the scale, offset, and margins you want for your hardcopy plots and printed listings. A slider control is provided where you may adjust the font size used for hardcopy printout.  You can also make fine adjustments to the position where the PAD dialog opens on your monitor.

 

Some printer drivers in Windows do not accurately scale plotted output.  To adjust the scale, make a hardcopy plot of one of the SYNOPSYS graphical formats, such as a DWG plot.  SYNOPSYS draws a border on the page, measuring exactly 9.5 by 7 inches.  Measure these distances, and if you find an error, calculate a scale factor that is the desired distance divided by the measured distance.  Enter this number into the boxes to multiply in X and Y where X is the 7-inch direction.  If the plot is not properly centered on the page, enter the desired offset, in inches, into the boxes labeled ‘Offset’.

 

Some printers will not print text closer to the edge of the paper than a certain amount.  You can enter a desired margin distance for all four sides of the page into the boxes labeled ‘margin’.

 

The printer font size may be adjusted with the slider between 20 characters/inch and 10 cpi.

 

The location and size of the PAD dialog is remembered after you close that dialog, and it should come back with the same size and location the next time you open it.  But the accuracy can vary, depending on your monitor resolution and settings.  If it does not open in exactly the same location on your screen, you can enter an appropriate value in the Offset amount in X and Y edit boxes.  A value of 50 will position the dialog about ½ inch to the right or down.
