9.0 IMAGE ANALYSIS ROUTINES SPECIAL SET

 

Three programs are available to analyze an image or image patch, MIT, DETECTOR and FOR. MIT is a dialog that can show the effects of geometric aberrations as well as diffraction on a variety of targets. The other programs are used, for example, to determine how much of the energy that reaches a detector actually originated from the part of object space that is conjugate to the detector, and how much came from outside that area because of diffraction effects or aberrations.  They are general-purpose image dissection routines that can calculate the energy within a slit or on a detector and determine how this amount changes with size or position.  The DETECTOR program, described below, lets you specify the exact detector geometry you want, while the FOR program will vary either the size or position of a variety of detector shapes in steps automatically, and produce a plot of the results.

 

Input for either case must be prepared in two stages: first, the image model must be generated, and then the detector geometry is specified for the image analysis.

 

The model may be prepared interactively with the dialog MMO, and the plotted analysis with the dialog MID.
