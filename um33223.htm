3.3.2.2.3 Toric Surfaces                                         Previous Next TOC

 

(These options go into an RLE or CHG file, and may be entered interactively with the dialog SPS.)

 

Toric surfaces (including cylinders) may be entered by

 

SN RD RY

 

SN TORIC RX

 

where RY is the radius in the YZ plane and RX is the radius about which the YZ curve is rotated to generate the toric surface.  If either RX or RY is zero, the corresponding radius will be infinite, giving a cylindrical surface.  To change a toric surface back to a rotationally symmetrical shape, enter

 

SN SPH

 

To vary these parameters, use mnemonics RD for RY and G 1 for RX in the PANT file.

 

The actual value of the G 1 variable is 0.5/RX, and that is the value you should ask for if you ever want to target the toric radius in the AANT file.  Suppose you want RX to equal 3.0 on surface 6, but you can't just change it to that value because doing so will cause ray failures.  In that case, you could add to the AANT file the operand

 

M 0.166666 1 A G 1 6.
