10.8.4 When correcting OPD's                                                Next Previous TOC

 

If substantial mechanical changes are made to a lens that was previously corrected with OPD's, the perturbed lens may have aberrations whose magnitude is thousands of waves.  These cases are sometimes difficult to reoptimize using OPD's, and the design should first be corrected reasonably well using ray intercepts.

 

When your lens seems to be optimized as far as it will go, try using combinations of transverse ray aberrations and OPD's at the same time.  This minimizes both the ray height and the area under the FAN curves.  To do so effectively, the weighting factors should usually be very different for the two.  An error of one wave in OPD might be fairly good, while an error of one inch in the ray intercept is very much worse.  A suitable OPD weighting can be calculated from the wavelength (in lens units) and FNUM as follows:

 

Focal:

 

WT = wavelength/(2.0*FNUM*(cos(alpha) – 1)), where alpha is the angle of the axial marginal ray at the image.

 

Afocal:

 

WT = 4.0*wavelength*FNUM.

 

The MOM dialog for a ready-made rayset, also found in the EE toolbar button Ready Made Raysets will calculate this weight for you when you select rayset number 8.
