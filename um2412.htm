2.4.1.2 Global position                                                        Previous Next TOC

 

If a surface is defined GLOBAL, its absolute position is entered in the coordinate system of surface number 1.  If this option is selected, all three tilt angles may be input on a single surface, along with decenters.  The Euler angles may be specified in any order, as explained in section 3.8.2. The program will not, however, automatically compute the reverse tilt and decenter for a subsequent surface.  In other words, a global tilt is a permanent tilt.  Global tilts cannot be picked up in the same way as can relative tilts, but a later surface may be made coincident with a previous surface, using the COINCIDENT specification described in section 3.8.2.1.  Some caution is needed when using global surfaces: if a surface is relative, and the surface before it is shifted along the axis because of a thickness change earlier on, the relative surface is shifted too, keeping the same relative spacing.  A global surface, on the other hand, will remain where it was relative to surface one, and will therefore appear at a new location relative to the previous one.  This effect can lead to unexpected changes in the lens if overlooked.  Individual global parameters may be accessed in AI and optimization with the mnemonics XG, YG, ZG, AG, BG, and GG.
