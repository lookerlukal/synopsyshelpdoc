15.2.1.3 Conditions                                                                Previous Next TOC

 

Some examples of condition phrases have been already given in the above paragraphs.  A condition may

 

     1.Change a lens parameter temporarily

 

     2. Define a ray.

 

Typing a sentence like

 

What are the conditions?

 

will list some of the currently valid formats.

 

Condition phrases must begin with a preposition:

 

At, for, if, assuming suppose, when, as, in

 

Ex: At full field in color 2

 

and they may change any lens parameter whose name is in the vocabulary:

 

If 4 TH equals 3.456

For a global alpha tilt of -45

when the Nd of element 4 = 1.517

at a wavelength of .656

 

and set any ray parameter:

 

At YEN = 1 and HBAR equal to -.75,

for full field

 

A ray parameter not defined within the sentence assumes default values: on-axis field, center of pupil, primary color.

 

Here, too, the lens data are redefined as necessary; the global condition requires that the surface in question be GLOBAL, and the Nd requires that the surface have a glass model.  These changes are automatically made, if necessary, and the lens file is restored when the sentence has been satisfied.
