15.1.1 Grammatical Considerations                                        Previous Next TOC

 

Natural language processing has proved to be one of the most intractable of programming tasks, and the SYNOPSYS AI program can, at best, process only a limited variety of sentences using a limited vocabulary.  Nonetheless, the number of sentences that are properly understood by SYNOPSYS, using a few simple grammar rules, is astonishing.  I hope that you will find this feature a valuable addition to the repertoire of SYNOPSYS commands, and that it will be of use whenever the normal commands and output are not sufficient.

 

The intent of the AI feature is to enable you to ask a question or change a lens parameter by means of a natural English sentence.  The vocabulary of Al is a few hundred words, and to get a listing of the current vocabulary, you may enter a sentence such as

 

List the subjects.

What are the verbs?

Conditions?

 

Input to AI may be in either upper or lower-case letters, and may include punctuation.  Using the words in the vocabulary lists, you may phrase your sentence following some simple rules:

 

1.

A sentence must have a subject and a verb. (A "?" at the end constitutes an implied verb.)

 

What is the radius of curvature of surface number 2?

2 RD?

4 TH?

Find the largest CAO.

Increase the field angle by 0.25

2 Nd = 1.517

tell me the value of aspheric coefficient 4 on surf 6.

Tilt surfaces 3 to 8 by 16.0

Change YMPI to 21.5

Print the SAG of surface 5 at a zone of 23.8

Put the stop on surface 2.

What is the thickness of lens number 4?

Change ID to "ID TRIPLET F/2.8"        (or set ID = "TRIPLET')

 

2.

Conditional phrases may be included, and will be interpreted as temporary changes to the lens or definitions of ray coordinates.

 

If the thickness of 3 is 23.6, what is the focal length?

At a field angle of 2.3, find YA on surface 23 for YEN equal 1.

I want to know the value of YA at full field.

YA AT HBAR = 1?

If HBAR = 1 and the relative aperture is 0.3, find the OPD.

If the stop is on 5, find the third-order coma.

 

3.

The word "and" can only join prepositional phrases.  Compound sentences, therefore, are not allowed.

 

4.

Normal SYNOPSYS commands may be used at will; AI will recognize them and send the request to the command interpreter.

 

Where is the stop?

PRT 6

STORE 8

Set G 6 on surface 2 = .001

 

5.

Words are parsed on 3 characters, unless confusion would result; then 4 characters are required.

 

find 3 TH.

FINAL 3             (In Al mode, "FIN" is ambiguous.)

 

6.

A sentence must fall completely on a single input line.

 

 

Error messages will explain any deviation from these rules.  If you leave out the verb, for example, the program will ask you for it.  You then need only enter the verb – the rest of the sentence is retained.  If you enter a word not in the vocabulary, the program will ask you to retype four characters beginning at that word.  If the new characters (or blanks) produce a valid sentence, it is processed.  If you get into a situation where the correct response to an error message is not clear, press <esc>, and Al will discard the sentence and start over.
