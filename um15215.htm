15.2.1.5 Looping in Al – Loop Verbs                                        Previous Next TOC

 

One of the most important uses of Al is to execute loops while changing and evaluating parameters.  The rules given above for asking questions and altering lens data apply, but the loop is implied by a loop verb rather than a question or change verb.  There are two basic forms: self-contained, and a MACro form.  The self-contained form is:

 

(loop verb) (subject) (subject) (parameter) (conditions)

 

Ex: Plot the SAG of surface 5 for Y = 0 to 25

Ex: print BACK versus SA3 as the wavelength varies from .3 to .8

 

Loop verbs are actually composite verbs

 

Plot … for … equals ... to …

Print … as … varies from … to …

Plot … versus … for ... = … to …

 

The several words in the above sentences are called the "loop verb".  Any lens or ray parameter that can be changed by a change verb, or controlled by a condition, may be used in that way in a loop sentence.

 

If a plot is requested, the data to be plotted are taken from the sentence itself; the subject becomes the ordinate, and the loop parameter becomes the abscissa – unless another subject has been requested by the "versus ..." form of loop verb.  In the latter case, the printed data give three columns (but the plot always shows just two parameters).

 

In the MACro form of loop, the data to be plotted are taken from the MACro itself, as well as from the AI sentence.

 

Do macro for th0 = 500 to 1000

 

The MACro in this case may contain ORD and/or ABS sentences, like

 

Ordinate back

abscissa equals coma.

 

Of course, in a simple case like this, the entire affair could be stated as a self-contained loop:

 

Plot BACK vs COM3 as the object dist varies from 500 to 1000.

 

A special form of loop is available for ZFILE zoom lenses.  In this case, one does not want to vary a parameter over a given range in a given number of steps; rather, one wants to vary the zoom position, which of course always must be an integer value.  The AI sentence takes the same form as described above, except that the loop variable is the word “ZOOM”.  Thus, if the first zoom group consists of surfaces 14 through 18, one could get a plot of the airspace before surface 14 with the sentence

 

 PLOT 13 TH FOR ZOOM = 1 TO 10.

 

(This assumes that 10 zooms have been defined.)

 

You can also put any evaluation you wish into a MACro, load or run the MACro once, and then issue a sentence such as

 

 DO MACRO FOR ZOOM = 3 TO 6.

 

(In this case we show how to evaluate only four of the zoom positions.)  The MACro might contain, for example

 

 RPT .005 10 M.

 

If you then enter DO MAC FOR ZOOM = 1 TO 10, you will get 10 rayfans plots, one for each zoom position.  Be aware that, for this to work, you must declare GAW before starting the loop (or click the “New Windows” button on the top toolbar), so each picture will show up in its own window, and not overwrite the previous one.  Used in this way, the AI loop ignores the STEPS setting, and runs the number of zoom cases requested.
