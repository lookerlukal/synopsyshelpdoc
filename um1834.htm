18.3.4 A more general nonsequential system                                Previous Next TOC

 

In the unusual system shown below, there are several nonsequential paths for certain rays.  Surface 1 is a LOOSE surface.  The lens surface 2, mirror surfaces 4 and 6, and lens surfaces 8 and 9 are all CAPTURE, and the mirrors are also LOOSE.  The bundle of rays labeled A go through the system in a normal way, but the rays in bundle B have a variety of histories.  The top two, for example, leave the system without hitting anything; the next two rays from the top enter surface 9 and go through the lens backwards, then reflect from mirror 6 and are lost because they miss mirror 4.  The bottom two rays of bundle B do not hit any surfaces at all.

 

 

The main difficulty in setting up a system like this is to remember to place the appropriate flags where needed.  If surface 8 were not CAPTURE, for example (light enters only from the normal direction, so you might think to leave it sequential), then light from the top part of surface 6 would not get into the lens.  This is because surface 6, the mirror, is LOOSE – and it can only send light to its neighbors or to a CAPTURE surface.  Surface 8 is not a neighbor of 6, and light from a LOOSE surface can never back up, as frequently happens in sequential systems when there is a dummy surface at a fold mirror.  So there is no path from 6 to 7 to 8 for rays hitting the top part (-Y on the surface) of surface 6.
