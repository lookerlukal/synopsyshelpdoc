A conic section may be defined by its major and minor axes as well as by its radius and conic constant. Appendix C describes the sign conventions that apply to the axes, which may be input with

 

SN B b A a.

 

(note order).  A conic section may be placed on a surface that is controlled by a curvature solve.

 

A conic constant lower than -1.0 makes the surface a hyperbola, and with a very high value can be used to model an axicon.  Between 0 and -1 models an ellipse, and a positive value models an oblate spheroid.
