5.41 Lens Spacer Length (SPACERS)                                Previous Next TOC

 

The command

 

 SPACERS [ JSSS JSPS ]

 

will calculate the length of the lens cell or spacer ring between two elements.  This calculation takes the value of each airspace and adjusts it by the sag of the surfaces on both sides.  The surface range may be entered with the command; the default covers the entire lens.  The calculation is done in the YZ plane and is not valid if the surfaces do not exhibit axial symmetry.  This feature may be accessed by the utility MUT.

 

The aperture used for the sag calculation on each surface is equal to one of the following;

 

1.  The value of any hard CAO in effect on the surface, if any.

2.  The value of EFILE points A or E, if no hard CAO has been declared.  Point E is taken for the lower surface number spanning the airspace, and point A for the higher.

3.  The current soft CAO if no EFILE or hard CAO data are in effect.

 8   9    16.2998    12.8906E    6.0611C    1.2865     0.0000    15.0133

 

Note: apertures marked "C" are taken from the current CAO value;

Apertures marked "E" are taken from the current EFILE data for points A or E.

SPACER is the airspace adjusted by the sags on both sides.

 

Suppose you want to design a spacer between surfaces 6 and 7.  The length of that spacer is 26.0963 mm, as given in the table.  The aperture taken for the sag calculation is at EFILE point E (12.6585) on surface 6 (noted by the tag “E”), while that for surface 7 is at the hard CAO (12.1) on that surface (noted by “C”).  Since the  EFILE data describe only the shape of the lens and do not specify the point on the surface where you want the spacer to touch the surface, we allow the hard CAO to override the EFILE points for this calculation.  If you want the EFILE points to govern, be sure to delete any hard CAO that may be in effect.
