Annotation Editor

 

The annotation editor can add text, lines, circles, rectangles, special characters, and arrows to a graphics picture.  To open the Afile toolbar, click when the picture is displayed.

 

To add an item to the display:

 

1. Click on the toolbar button controlling the item you want to add.

 

2. Click in the drawing where you want the item to appear.

 

3. For lines, circles, rectangles, and arrows, drag the mouse to the desired endpoint of the item.

 

4. To hide a portion of the drawing or displayed text, click the Hide button and then drag over the area to hide.

 

5. Release the mouse button.

 

If you are adding a line of text, a dialog will open where you can type the desired character string and select the font size.  Several frequently used words are shown on buttons, along with special symbols, such as ±, which often appear on element and assembly drawings.  Just click on one of the words or symbols, and it will be typed for you in the edit box.  If your text will extend over more than a single line, click the "Multiline" checkbox (so it is selected).  Now type the text you want to go on the picture.  If you do not select the Multiline checkbox, pressing the Enter key is the same as clicking on the OK button.  If it is selected, the Enter key goes to the next line.  If you have defined any of the nine Annotation Strings, clicking the appropriate button displayed in

 

 

loads the defined character string into the edit box.

 

The WAVELENGTHS button creates a table showing the current wavelengths and weights.  This is useful for annotating a plot prepared by those features that do not automatically put the information on the picture, such as AI plots.

 

Your text will hide any lines that are below it.  This is so that when you superimpose your text on a dimension arrow, the arrow will have a hole for it.  If you want a somewhat larger hole, select the “Pad text endpoints” checkbox.  This will add three blank characters before and after your text.

 

You can copy one or more lines of text from the Command Window and paste them into the Annotation text dialog.  Suppose your lens has a DOE and you want to display the coefficients on an ELD drawing.  First, do a SPEC so the coefficients are displayed in the window.  Then drag over the lines you want to copy, and press Ctrl+C.  Now click to open the Annotation editor (if it is not open already), and then click the same button in the Annotation toolbar.  Now click in the drawing where you want the annotation to go, and type Ctrl+V.  Your text is in shown in the window, and when you click OK it is displayed on the drawing.  You have just saved a lot of typing.

 

Other buttons in the Afile toolbar let you add hashmarks to a lens profile, as recommended by the ISO standards.  These come in two sizes; for a smaller version, hold down the <Ctrl> key as you click on the drawing.  Another symbol used by the ISO standard is the diamond, which follows similar rules.

 

You can modify an item that has been added by clicking on the button .  Each item will then display one or two edit handles.  To move an item, click and drag the handle at the starting point of the item.  To resize an item, click and drag the item at the end point.  To modify a line of text, double-click its handle.  To delete an item entirely, click a handle and then press the <delete> key.

 

You can select the color of items you add to the picture by clicking on one of the buttons before you add the item.

 

The annotation you have entered can be saved and fetched with the buttons and .  It will also appear on printed graphics hardcopy, but not on files saved with the HARDCOPY commands.  It can also be saved along with the picture in which it is embedded with the toolbar button in the Graphics Window.
