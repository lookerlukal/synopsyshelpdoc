3.3.2.1 Spherical Surfaces                                Previous Next TOC

 

(These options go into an RLE or CHG file, and may be entered interactively with the dialog SPS.)

 

SN { RD / RAD } NB

radius of curvature.  This entry will make the base curve spherical.  If the radius value exceeds 1.0E10, the surface will be considered flat.

SN CV NB

reciprocal radius, or curvature.  This also makes the base curve spherical or flat.

 

SN PCV NB [ M [ B ] ]

pickup curvature of surface NB.  A negative value of NB reverses the curvature, and the constants M and B may be used to scale and offset the results.  (CV = CV(NB)*M + B)  Any type of surface shape may be picked up except HOEs, DOEs, and GRATINGs, but the parameters M and B affect only the spherical and conic parameters a and c (see App. C).

 

SN UMC NB

solves for the spherical curvature giving the specified value of the paraxial axial marginal ray angle with respect to the optical axis, after refraction or reflection at this surface. NB is the tangent of the desired angle.  To yield a desired F/number, enter (1/2FNUM) for NB.  The angle (see section 2.5.4) will have the same sign as NB.

 

SN UPC NB

curvature solve for the paraxial principal ray angle after refraction or reflection. NB is the tangent of the desired angle.  To produce a telecentric system, for example, you would enter "UPC 0".

 

SN YMC NB

curvature solve for the paraxial marginal ray height on the next surface.

 

SN YPC NB

curvature solve for the paraxial principal ray height on the next surface.

 

SN NCOP

removes any curvature pickups or solves on this surface, keeping the latest value.

 

VMC, VPC, XMC, XPC

are used in the same way as UMC, UPC, YMC, and YPC, except that they refer to the X-Z plane rather than the Y-Z plane.  These options produce a spherical surface, but the (paraxial) effects of preceding non-spherical surfaces are taken into account.  In the event of a conflict, the Y-Z solves take precedence over the X-Z.  Entering any of these options automatically turns on the XPXT option.

 

SN AMY

turns on the aplanatic solve for the specified surface based on the marginal ray.

 

SN APY

turns on the aplanatic solve for that surface based on the principal ray.

 

SN IMY NB

is an angle of incidence solve for the marginal ray.

 

SN IPY NB

is an angle of incidence solve for the principal ray.

SN CCY

gives a surface concentric about the principal ray.

 

SN AMX, APX, IMX, IPX, CCX

are used in the same way as AMY, APY, IMY, IPX, and CCY, except that they refer to the X-Z plane rather than the Y-Z plane.  In the event of a conflict, the Y-Z solve takes precedence over the X-Z solve.

 

The above options control the spherical radius of the entered surface.  Most of the aspheric surface shape options cannot be assigned to such a surface.  You are permitted, however, to add a conic section, power-series terms of the form DC1, etc., or a Zernike polynomial to a surface controlled by a solve.
