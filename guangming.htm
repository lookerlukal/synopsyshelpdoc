Chinese Chengdu Guangming Glasses (Section G)                                Previous Next TOC

 

This Chinese company has adopted the strange practice of calling many of their glasses by the same names found in the Schott catalog.  Unfortunately, the optical properties of these glasses are very different from each other, even though the names are identical.  To prevent unintended use of the wrong glass – which is difficult to avoid otherwise – we have prefixed most of the Chinese names with "G-" followed by the name used in their catalog.  Those glasses designated by the vendor as environment-friendly are instead indicated by the prefix "H-", in keeping with their catalog.  We recommend that you add a note to element drawings clearly specifying this glassmaker when you use these glasses – to prevent confusion with the Schott glass types.
