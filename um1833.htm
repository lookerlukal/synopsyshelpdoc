18.3.3 Segmented mirror                                                        Previous Next TOC

 

Here is a somewhat more complicated system, where the segmented surface is a mirror rather than a window, as in the previous example.  In this system, shown below, surfaces 2 and 3 are the mirror segments, positioned with GLOBAL coordinates to simulate a small position error on surface 3.  Surface 1 is LOOSE, as in the window example, and 2 and 3 are CAPTURE, as is surface 5, the entrance side of the singlet lens before the image.

 

In this kind of system it is of course necessary to turn off paraxial raytracing with the NPXT option; and since there is a gap between the mirror segments, causing the chief ray to fail to trace, we also must use the ICR option to specify a chief ray at a suitable zone.
