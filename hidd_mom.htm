MOM – Parameter Definition Menu

 

This dialog is reached from the MOM dialog by clicking on ‘Define Variables’.  There are three sections:

 

Top section

Here you see a drawing of the lens along with three category buttons labeled ‘Radii’, ‘Spacings’, and ‘Glass Model’.  To create a variable for optimization, just click a category and then click on the vertex of the surface you want to vary.  You can click ‘All’ or ‘None’ to vary all or none of those variables, and you can change the way the lens drawing appears with the buttons to the right of the picture.  Variables entered in this way will use the default limits, described in section 10.2 of the User’s Manual.

 

Middle section

In this section you can select variables for which you need to enter your own limits.  The variable name can be found in the drop-down boxes, and you must click on a ‘USE’ box to activate that variable.

 

Bottom section

Here you can select from a category of variables which require an index number in addition to the usual surface number.  These include group tilt and decenter variables, where you give the number of surfaces in the group, as well as G variables, which refer to aspheric terms and a variety of other parameters, where you give the G-array index.

 

There are other variable types that you can enter yourself, usually into an optimization MACro.  Click on the User’s Manual link, above, for a complete list.
