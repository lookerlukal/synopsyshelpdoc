10.7.2.1 Examples of Multiple Configuration Input                        Next Previous TOC

 

 

Consider a three-element zoom lens giving a zoom ratio of 4:1.  Although the ZFILE option is actually easier, we will show here how to design it using the ACON feature.  We desire to vary all of the radii and spacings, keeping the focal plane in a fixed position with respect to element 1 in both modes.  In order to make the speed of both lenses the same, we input entrance pupil beam sizes having a 4:1 ratio, and field angles with a 1:4 ratio.  If we now require identical Gaussian image heights in both modes (in the AANT file), the desired zoom ratio will result, and the F/numbers will be the same.  Paraxial YMT solves were used in both modes, which are already in ACONs 1 and 2.

 

ACON 2 PICKUPS

1 PCV 1

2 PCV 2

3 PCV 3

4 PCV 4

5 PCV 5

6 PCV 6

END

 

PANT

VLIST RAD 1 2 3 4 5 6

VLIST TH 2 4

ACON 2

VLIST TH 2 4

END

 

AANT

ACON 1

…                        image quality aberrations for configuration 1

M 1.6 1 A GIHT

M 4.82 1 A TOTL

A BACK

 

ACON 2

…                        image quality aberrations for configuration 2

M 1.6 1 A GIHT

M 4.82 1 A TOTL

A BACK

END

 

SYNO 15 MULTI

 

 

This example illustrates one method by which a lens may be moved while maintaining the same separations between its neighbors in two zoom positions.  Assume the separation between surfaces 9 and 12 is to be variable but the same for both zooms.  The position of surface 10 is allowed to vary.  We would input

 

PANT

VY 9 TH

VY 11 TH

ACON 1

VY 9 TH

VY 11 TH

…                        other variables

END

 

AANT

…                        image-quality aberrations

M 0 1

A TH 9

A TH 11

ACON 2

S TH 9

S TH 11

END

 

The sum of airspaces 9 and 11 will be the same in both modes.  (Of course, for this simple example, the ZFILE zoom option is even simpler.)

 

 

In this example, we wish to optimize a single lens for two object distances, using the middle two elements as the focusing members.  In ACON 1, suppose we entered

 

OBB 0 5 1.6 0

8 YMT

 

and in ACON 2

 

OBC 60 5 1.6 0

 

The alternate now has an object at 60 inches, subtending a field of 5 degrees at the vertex of surface 1.  In the PANT file, we will vary all parameters of ACON 1 (except 8 TH, of course), and they will be picked up in the alternate with

 

ACON 2 PICKUPS

ZOOM

2 PTH 0                (note that we remove these two thicknesses

6 PTH 0                from the pickup list so they can be varied

END                        independently.)

 

We will include in the PANT file

 

ACON 1

VLIST RAD 1 2 3 4 5 6 7 8

VLIST TH 2 6

ACON 2

VLIST TH 2 6

 

and include in the AANT file

 

ACON 1

M 0 1 A TH 2

A TH 6

ACON 2

S TH 2

S TH 6

 

The middle two elements in both configurations will be adjusted in focus so as to maintain an optimum image quality in both configurations.
