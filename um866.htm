8.6.6 Coefficients of Wavefront Polynomial (COE)         Next Previous TOC

 

The coefficients of an interpolation polynomial may be calculated for use by DIFF. This feature runs faster for certain diffraction analysis than the option requiring DPF, and it also permits some user control over the individual terms.  See section 9.2 for more information. The input is:

 

COE { ICOL / P } HBAR 0 0 GBAR [ FULL ].

 

The program COE traces a grid of 21 by 21 rays and solves for the coefficients with a least-squares method.  Apertures and vignetting are considered.  The polynomial used by COE is the same as that described in section 3.3.2.2.2.

 

The FULL option itemizes the coefficients and lists the polynomial term for each, as shown below:

 

     COEFFICIENT  POLYNOMIAL

 ---------------------------

 

 1  -0.972493     R**2

 2   0.716866E-02 Y

 3   0.383261     R**4

 4   0.478242     Y*R**2

 5   0.127801     Y**2

 6  -0.353496E-01 R**6

 7  -0.418757     Y*R**4

 8   0.670004     R**2*Y**2

 9  -0.110492     Y**3

10  -0.716602E-01 R**8

11  -0.342059E-03 X

12   0.144931E-02 X*R**2

13  -0.169279E-02 X*R**4

14   0.326432E-03 X**3

15   0.258674E-03 X*Y

16   0.155731E-01 R**10

 

Individual terms in the expansion may be controlled in the AANT file with the mnemonic COE.

 

The PUPIL feature will model the wavefront with the COE coefficients instead of with real raytracing if the COE command immediately precedes the PUPIL command.
