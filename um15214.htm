15.2.1.4 Change Verbs                                                        Previous Next TOC

 

Change verbs are used in a sentence that has the structure:

 

(change verb) (subject) (value)

 

The condition phrases described above have already used with some of the change verbs.  These imply changes to the lens:

 

=, equal, equal to, make, make equal to

change to, change by

increase to, increase by

decrease to, decrease by.

 

Ex: Increase 5 RAD by .1

Ex: make surface 3 flat

 

Some of the change verbs must first evaluate the quantity to be changed, and then change it.  Unlike the conditional changes described above, changes made by a change verb are permanent alterations to the lens.  A surface may become GLOBAL or LOCAL, or get a glass model index as a result.  If you ask Al to "increase the TH of surface 6 to 12.5", and surface 6 presently has a thickness of 15.5, the request will give an error message, and no change will be made (because the requested value is not an increase).

 

Not all valid nouns may be changed by Al; you could not, for example, instruct the program to “decrease the spherical aberration by 10.0", since this is not an independent quantity.
