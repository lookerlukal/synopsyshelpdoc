10.3.14 Automatic Aperture Control (AAC, ALA)                                Next Previous TOC

 

Often one would like the lens to fit inside a fixed diameter cell.  One can of course give a target value to individual lens apertures with the CAO aberration and keep things under control – but there is an easier way that is especially useful when you cannot predict which element will tend to become too large.  This is the AAC aberration.  The format is

 

 AAC [ TARGET WEIGHT [ WINDOW ] ].

 

The program will monitor the default CAO on every surface (not just those that are variables) and add a penalty to the merit function that depends on the magnitude of the aperture size violation.  The penalty goes as the square of the violation if the error is less than WINDOW, and linearly thereafter, and the violation is just the current CAO value minus the TARGET.  Surfaces assigned a hard aperture are not included in this evaluation.

 

This is one of several kinds of monitors that operate continuously during optimization.

 

This aberration is calculated after most other aberrations, and the lens is analyzed over all zoom positions and for the most recently specified ACON.

 

A related form uses the mnemonic ALA instead of AAC and applies only to lens surfaces, not mirrors.  This can be useful when you want to add corrector lenses to a catadioptric system, and the mirrors can be larger than the lenses.
