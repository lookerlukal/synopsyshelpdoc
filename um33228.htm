3.3.2.2.8 Aspheric Toroids                                Previous Next TOC

 

(These options go into an RLE or CHG file, and may be entered interactively with the dialog SPS.)

 

An aspheric toroid is a surface whose shape in the Y-Z plane is given by a conic section, flat, or sphere, plus optional power-series terms using the input DC1 and DC2 (defined as described below) -- and whose shape in the X-Z plane is found by rotating the Y-Z curve about a given point on the axis.  The Y-Z shape is thus aspheric, while the X-Z curve is spherical.  To enter this kind of surface, give the Y-Z shape first, using the mnemonics RD, RAD, CV, CC, DC1, and DC2, and then specify the center of curvature for the X-Z curve with

 

SN ASTORIC RX

 

where RX is the radius of curvature in the X-Z plane. (RX = 0 sets the curvature to infinity.)  The X-Z curvature is placed into the G(17) aspheric location, and may be varied by giving that term as a variable in optimization.

 

The aspheric coefficients used for ASTORICs differ from those of ordinary aspheres; only terms in even powers of Y enter into the equation, since the profile thus described is applied to the Y-Z shape only, where X equals zero.  The terms in powers of X are replaced by additional powers of Y, giving terms up to order 20 in Y:
