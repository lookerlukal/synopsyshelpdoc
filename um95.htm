9.5 Image Illumination (ILLUM)                                        Next Previous TOC

 

The command

 

ILLUM NRYS { HBAR / P } [ GBAR ]

 

will calculate the relative illumination at the entered field point, or plot a curve of 21 field points at the entered skew field point GBAR if the optional “P” is entered.

 

This feature calculates the solid angle of the converging pencil at the given image point, multiplies by the cosine of the obliquity angle of the central ray at the image surface, and then traces the requested number of rays to determine the vignetting of the pupil and transmission of the ray bundle according to the polarization state and coatings that may be assigned to the lens surfaces.  Then the absorption along the path of each ray is calculated and factored in.  The resulting quantity is then normalized by the results at the on-axis image point.  Rotational symmetry is not assumed, and the entire analysis will use the entered GBAR parameter, if any, taking the HBAR points from the axis to a value of HBAR = 1.

 

If a plot is requested, the program will add a message warning you if the program was unable to consider polarization, vignetting, or absorption.

 

This feature may also be accessed via the MTR and MGI dialogs.  A separate feature, IPAT, will display the illumination pattern at the final surface.
