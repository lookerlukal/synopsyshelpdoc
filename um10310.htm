10.3.10 Composite aberrations                                                Next Previous TOC

 

The aberration definitions given in the previous sections permit you to control simple algebraic combinations of the specified quantities, such as ray differences, proportions, and so on.  When a more general expression is required, another form of input is more convenient.  A composite aberration may be defined with

 

M TAR WT COMPOSITE

CD1 aberration                (composite definition 1, etc.)

CD2 aberration

CD3 aberration

…

 

CD9 aberration

= equation

 

This format accepts an equation as part of the aberration definition much as it would be expressed in Fortran, with numeric constants, the Zn variables, arithmetic signs ( +, -, *, /, ** ), parentheses, ABS, the SQRT and circular functions SIN, ASIN, COS, ACOS, TAN, ATAN, and the exponential and logarithmic functions EXP, ALOG, and ALOG10.  Angular arguments in every case are in units of radians, as in Fortran.

 

Up to 9 variables may be defined (in order, starting with CD1) in the same way as other aberrations, given in the preceding sections of this manual – and then used at will in the equation.  For example, the input

 

M 1.5 1 COMPOSITE

CD1 2 YA 0 0 1 0 3

CD2 SAG 3 0 1.2

CD3 SA3

= 2.0*SQRT( CD1*CD1 + CD2*CD2 ) / CD3

 

will perform the stated calculation, where CD1 is the YA coordinate on surface 3 of the axial marginal ray, CD2 is the sag of that surface at the 1.2 zone, and CD3 is the third-order spherical aberration.  The results of the calculation will be compared to the target value of 1.5, and the difference will go into the merit function.  Note that the equation itself appears on a separate line that starts with the equals sign “=”.  That character causes the line to bypass the normal command interpreter, which cannot interpret the math symbols.

 

Operators are taken from right to left, with the precedence

 

**        exponentiation

*, /        multiplication, division

+,-        addition, subtraction

 

Parentheses may be used at will to force precedence.  For example, the above equation is different from

 

= 2.0*SQRT ( ( CD1*CD1 + CD2*CD2 ) / CD3 ).

 

The CDs, when defined in an AANT file, retain their last value until redefined – so they may be used again in another equation. See the next section for a related feature, the Zn parameters.

 

The AI program can also perform calculations with these quantities, outside of an optimization run, and it can also show you the value of all of the current CDn parameters.  See section 15.1.4.

 

Note that the CDs can only be defined with a self-contained aberration.  This means, for example, that you could not use a GNR, GNN, and so on, within the definition, since those generate a whole ray set that COMPOSITE cannot evaluate.

 

Be aware that the operators ASIN, ACOS, and ATAN deliver the answer in radians, not degrees.  Also, note that the equals sign, "=" is never permitted in command strings, even in comments.
