3.3.2.2.5 Spline Surfaces                                Previous Next TOC

 

(These options go into an RLE or CHG file, and may be entered interactively with the dialog SPS.)

 

A very general form of surface curvature designation may be obtained using the spline option.  With this feature, the surface is defined by giving a set of (Y,Z) coordinates at various zones, and the program will fill in the rest of the curve with either a piecewise-cubic interpolation or a linear interpolation, depending on user input.  Up to 25 zones may be described, and the surface is always assumed rotationally symmetric.  The Y's must be in increasing order; the axis point (Y = 0) is automatically assumed, and the slope at that point is always zero. This point must not appear in the data.

 

The surface may have a RAD and CC in addition to the spline.  PXT takes the curvature from the first zone and adds that to the entered CV if any.  The input is:

 

SN SPLINE N [ LINEAR ]

 

Y1 Z1

 

Y2 Z2

 

…

 

YN ZN.

 

The optional LINEAR results in a linear interpolation.  To vary the (Y,Z) coordinates of point number J during optimization, use the input (in the PANT file) VY SN G NB, where NB is given by (2J-1) for Y and (2J) for Z.

 

To remove a spline designation, the surface must first be NULLed.

 

Another form of spline curve is available in the form of a NURBS surface, in two versions: axially symmetric and non-symmetric.  These are found as USS types 14 and 15.
