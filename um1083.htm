10.8.3 High-order aspherics                                                Next Previous TOC

 

Because of the high power to which the aperture is taken when terms such as G3, G6, G10, and G16 are put on a surface, certain numerical problems may sometimes occur.  If the magnitude of the aperture is very different from unity, switch 7 should be on so that the derivative increments are reasonable.  In extreme cases, such as when the aperture is expressed as a large number of millimeters, the optimization process may involve manipulating numbers which differ by as much as 15 orders of magnitude, causing numerical problems in the optimization that may result in failure to use the terms properly.  It is then helpful to scale the system to units of meters, perform the optimization, and then scale it back.
