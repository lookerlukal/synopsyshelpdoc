5.12 Gaussian Beam Trace (BEAM)                Next Previous TOC

 

The path of a beam that has been described by the object designation OBG in the RLE or CHG file (see section 3.1.2) may be analyzed with the input

 

[X]BEAM.

 

At each surface the program prints the local beam radius, waist location measured from that surface (projecting the local beam backward or forward as necessary), the waist radius, and the beam divergence.  The calculation is performed paraxially, so aberrations, tilts and decenters, and vignetting are not considered.  The divergence is the tangent of the ray angle.  If the optional XBEAM form is used, the analysis is in the X-Z plane instead of the Y-Z.

 

The output properties of a Gaussian beam may be controlled in optimization.  See Section 10.3.2.

 

BEAM is of course a first-order analysis.  To see higher-order effects, look at real ray paths (if the system obeys real-ray optics after surface 1) or DPROP if diffraction is an issue at places other than the entering beam waist location.
