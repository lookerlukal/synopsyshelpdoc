WorkSheet: Fold Here

 

Clicking this button will prompt for an angle and direction, and then will insert a fold mirror and a dummy surface for coordinate rotation at the next place where you click in an airspace.  An angle of zero degrees folds the light directly back on itself, while 90 degrees is a right-angle fold.  You can determine which way to fold most easily by looking at the circled surface numbers on the PAD drawing: these are normally at the +Y edge of the surface.

 

You can easily change the sign of the tilt angle later with the button , and you can adjust the tilt angle value with the slider normally used for CV.

 

Caution is advised if your system is already folded elsewhere; if you click in a large airspace, the program attempts to determine between which two surfaces you have clicked -- and the click point may sometimes actually be nearer to the vertex of a different surface entirely.  In this case, the fold mirror will not go where you intended.  The solution is 1) to click close to a desired vertex (you can slide the mirror into its desired location later), and 2) be sure to make a checkpoint before you use this button.

 

Caution is also advised if you insert a fold mirror before surface 1.  If the object is at infinity, this works well enough – although the lens drawings are oriented in the coordinate system of the (new) surface 1, which is of course at an angle, which you probably don’t want.  But if the object is finite, the value of TH0 and YMP1 must also change, and the program does not do that for you.  The moral is, if you want to insert a fold mirror in front of your lens, add a dummy surface there first (and adjust the object if necessary) and then insert the fold mirror in the space following the new surface 1.

 

If you plane to insert a fold mirror just before the start of a ZFILE zoom group, it is also wise to first insert a dummy surface at the desired location.  The program by default automatically assigns the inserted surface as part of that zoom group (which is how AEI can add elements to a group) -- but in this case you probably don't want the mirror to be included.  So, after you insert the dummy surface, edit the zoom parameters to remove that surface from the group that follows it, and then fold at that dummy surface.

 

You can also fold a system in command mode.  See FOLD.
