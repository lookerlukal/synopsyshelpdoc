5.19 FOLD, UNFOLD                                Next Previous TOC

 

It is occasionally necessary to insert or remove a fold mirror from a lens system.  Although this can be done with the ordinary lens changing options, the effort is tedious since the sign of all of the thicknesses following such a change must be reversed.  The simplest way is with PAD and WorkSheet, where you select the toolbar button to fold or unfold and then simply click on the lens axis to insert or remove a mirror at that point.

 

Another way is to enter the command

 

 FOLD SN ANGLE [ BETA ]

 MFOLD SN ANGLE [ BETA ]

 

or        UNFOLD SN.

 

To use the FOLD command, SN must currently be a flat dummy surface (no index break) and the next surface must also be a flat dummy coincident with SN.  Both SN and the next surface must be free of tilts and decenters, although a tilt with a group size of 1 is allowed.  Surface SN will be changed to a reflecting surface with an alpha tilt of minus one-half of ANGLE, and the following surface will get the other half of the angle.  The signs of subsequent thicknesses and curvatures are reversed.

 

The second surface is assigned the PAS option, which picks up the tilt of the first; thus if you change the first tilt angle, the second automatically follows along.  This is also the result when you use the WorkSheet option that inserts the required two surfaces for you.

 

If word four of the command is BETA, the tilt will be created in the X-direction instead of Y.

 

The MFOLD form of the command requires only a single dummy surface at SN.  It creates the MAT or MBT form of mirror tilt, where the following surface is assigned an equal tilt as a form of negative undo.  This form does not require a separate dummy surface with a PAS option assigned.

 

The UNFOLD command will unfold the system at surface SN, provided that the system currently appears to be folded at that point.  This means that either surface SN and the following one must have equal alpha tilts, and surface SN must be a reflector, or else surface SN is currently assigned a mirror tilt of the form MAT or MBT.
