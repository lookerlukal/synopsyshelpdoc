8.6.3 Diffraction MTF                                         Next Previous TOC

 

Two forms of diffraction MTF are available.  The first uses the Hopkins convolution algorithm and the second a Fourier-transform method.  The former is preferred for most lenses, since it remains accurate up to several tens of wavelengths of aberration, while the latter requires a well-corrected image to ensure complete sampling over a limited sample area.  The transform method has the significant advantage, however, that it can perform a vector-diffraction analysis, accounting for the effects of polarization, while the convolution method assumes that scalar diffraction theory holds.  Also, the wide-array version (WDMTF) plots the MTF at 45 degrees in addition to the sagittal and tangential MTFs, which is occasionally useful.  The dialog MOP gives a list of several MTF options that can be run interactively.  The command-mode formats are explained in the following sections.

 

If switch 87 is on, all of the MTF plots will show grid lines; if off, only the fiducials along the axes are drawn.
