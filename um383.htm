3.8.3. Local Coordinates                                        Previous Next TOC

 

The input required to enter local coordinates (see 2.4.1.3) of a surface is nearly the same as that for global, described in the previous section, and follows the same pattern:

 

LOCAL data may also be edited in the SPS dialog.  In an RLE or CHG file, a surface may be defined LOCAL with input of the form

 

 

SN LOCAL

or

SN LOCAL POSITION XL YL ZL

and

SN LOCAL ANGLE AL BL GL

 

The position and angles in this case are in the coordinates of the surface before surface SN.  The AI program can also access the mnemonics (XL -- GL) individually.

 

These quantities can be varied in the PANT file, and targeted in the AANT file, with the mnemonics

 

AL        local angle alpha

BL        local angle beta

GL        local angle gamma

 

XL        local position X

YL        local position Y

ZL        local position Z  .

 

Here, too, the Euler-angle order may be specified by substituting for the mnemonic ANGLE one of ABG, AGB, BAG, BGA, GAB, or GBA.
