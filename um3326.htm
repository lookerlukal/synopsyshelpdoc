3.3.2.6 Noncircular-zone surfaces (NCZONE)        Previous Next TOC

 

(These options go into an RLE or CHG file, and may be entered interactively with the dialog SPS.)

 

In systems such as a reflective Schmidt telescope with a tilted corrector plate, it is sometimes necessary to describe an aspheric surface with noncircular zones.  A surface designated with

 

 SN NCZONE COSPHI

 

is treated as a flat surface with power-series coefficients of the form DC1, etc., where the expansion is evaluated at the actual X coordinate of a ray but at a Y coordinate reduced by the factor COSPHI, which would ordinarily be the cosine of the tilt angle of the corrector plate.  The shape of the zones is thus elliptical, with the Y-dimension larger than the X-dimension for values of COSPHI less than unity.  The actual coefficients are entered with DC1, etc., as for normal power-series surfaces, and varied with the usual G-terms.
