15.2.1.2 Nouns – Subjects                                                        Previous Next TOC

 

All of the normal lens data are recognized by name, and many words have synonyms:

 

RD, RAD, radius of curvature, CV, curvature, conic constant

aspheric coefficient, G

TH, thickness

INDEX, Nd, Vd, Nl, N2, N3, N4, N5 etc.

CAO, clear aperture, RAO

SAG

tilt, global alpha tilt, AG, local beta tilt, BL, etc.

X position, global Y coordinate, YG, local Z position, ZL, etc.

 

If the subject refers to a specific surface, the surface number must be identified in some way:

 

at surface number 5

on surface 23

of 16

9

of element number 3

 

Ex: What is the clear aperture of surface 6?

Ex: Thickness of element 2?

Ex: 2 TH?

 

But:you cannot use 'FOR surface...', except when stating a condition, as discussed below.  If you refer to an element number, Al finds the surface number for you before going further.

 

Some of the above nouns carry subtle implications: if you ask for the ZG (global Z-coordinate) of a surface, but the lens is not currently in GLOBAL mode, it will temporarily be put into that mode, the question will be answered, and then it will be put back.

 

Other parameters, that do not refer to a surface, are also recognized:

 

stop, aperture stop, stop diameter, stop radius, real stop

aberrations, chromatic aberration, PAC, coma, TI3, etc.

object distance, THO, object height, object angle, UPPO, etc.

focal distance, FOCL, BACK, FNUM, F#, TOTL, DELF, etc.

units, wavelengths

MXSF

 

Ex: What is full field?

 

Most of the data obtained by tracing a ray are recognized by name, using the mnemonics given in Chapter 10, under ray aberrations:

 

XA, YA, ZA, XC, YC, OPD, UNI, HH, ZZ, XG, ZZG, HHG, XL, YL,

 

ZL, HHL, ZZL, ray angle, incidence angle

 

HBAR, GBAR, XEN, YEN, relative aperture, relative field

 

Ex: Find UNI on surface 6 at full aperture and HBAR = 1.

 

When the question refers to a quantity evaluated at a surface, AI can find the largest or smallest value:

 

Where is the largest CAO?

Find the smallest YA at full aperture.
