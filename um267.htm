2.6.7 Display of Vignetted Pupil                                                Previous Next TOC

 

The question naturally arises: when using one of the options that reduce the size of the entrance pupil in accordance with a vignetting table (VFIELD) or by searching for the pupil size that will pass through the hard apertures (WAP options), should one treat the reduced pupil as the “full aperture” pupil, – or should one show it as a smaller portion of the paraxial pupil on ray fans plots?

 

The choice is made with mode-control switch 59.  If this switch is on, the axis of RPT and RPO displays, as well as the PAD display, is the nominal paraxial pupil size, and the plotted curves will be shortened according to the reduction in pupil size produced by the above options.  If this switch is off, the axis extends only over the reduced pupil.
