2.4 Coordinate systems                                                Previous Next TOC

 

Every surface in a lens system has its own coordinate system.  The vertex of each surface is at the (0,0,0) point in this system, and the (X,Y,Z) axes are oriented as shown below.  As indicated in the figure, these form a left-handed cartesian system.  Light is normally assumed incident from the left (from the -z direction).

 

You may optionally request that the lens follow a right-handed system, with the entry

 

RHAND

 

in the RLE or CHG file.  To change to the default, use LHAND. In right-handed mode, the program will reverse the sign of every user entry that refers to an X or ZZ coordinate, and also reverse the sign of every output quantity that is affected, such as centroid positions, aspheric terms in odd powers of X, etc. The effect is to emulate a right-handed system, for the convenience of the user. Angle definitions are not affected by this option, however, a positive angle is always defined as an anti-clockwise rotation as seen from the positive end of the (left handed) axis.
