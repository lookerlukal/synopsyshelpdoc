6.8 Feathering Point (FEATHER)                        Next Previous TOC

 

The Y-coordinate at which two surfaces cross (feather) may be found with the input

 

FEATHER SN [ HAAP [ PLOT ] ]

 

where SN is the first surface of the two, and HAAP is an optional first approximation to the feathering point for systems with wild curvatures or more than one crossing.

 

The optional PLOT in word 4 makes a plot showing the sags of the two surfaces and the sag difference, out to the point HAAP.  Where the latter curve crosses the zero point is a feathering point.

 

An example FEATHER output follows. FEATHER may be run interactively from the dialog MUT.

 

SYNOPSYS AI>FEATHER 1

ID TRIPLET                                4111            15-FEB-98   16:20:49

 

FEATHERING ANALYSIS

WARNING: Feather calculation ignores tilts and decenters.

 

SURFACES  1 -  2 FEATHERED AT Y-HEIGHT OF     18.27979

 

SURF   SAG AT FEATHERING PT

___________________________

  1           7.24235

  2           0.30180
