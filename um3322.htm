3.3.2.2 Aspheric Surfaces                                Previous Next TOC

 

Aspherics are divided into seven categories: conic sections, power-series aspherics, torics, biconics, biradial conics, aspheric toroids, unusual surrace shapes, and splines.

 

In addition to the aspheric shapes described in the above sections, all aspherics can in addition have added to them a General Purpose Cylinder (GPCYLINDER).  This option is intended primarily for tolerancing, when one would like to find the tolerance limit on figure errors, which are modeled as a weak cylinder added to the base shape.  Since some of the USS shapes do not have a cylinder term in the formulation, this declaration adds the required capability.

 

The format is

 

sn GPCYLINDER sag

 

in the RLE or CHG file, where sag is the surface sag at unit aperture due to the added cylinder term.  The program adds a positive sag difference in X, and negative in Y, which models a saddle-shaped figure error, which is the same as a cylinder with the average defocus taken out.  (This is so the refocusing feature of BTOL cannot compensate for this figure error.)

 

This declaration cannot be assigned to spherical surfaces.

 

To illustrate, we add a flat plate behind a singlet lens, and assign a weak cylinder to the first side:

 

CHG

3 GPCYLINDER 1.0E-6

END

Now we examine the figure on surface 3.

 

FRINGES

SPROFILE 3 1.0E-5
