5.53 Sag Contribution from a Single G-term                                         Previous TOC

 

When designing a system with aspheric coefficients, one often would like to know if the resulting terms are really necessary.  There are several ways to find out.  The simplest is to make a checkpoint in PAD, and then zero out a suspect term in the WorkSheet.  Then click Update and see what effect the change has on the image.  It is also easy to find out whether a given aspheric term will help before you add it to the lens.  That question is addressed by AGT.

 

This section describes another feature, which can list the actual contribution of a given aspheric term and show the linear sag and the fringe contribution in double pass.  The idea is that if those contributions are vanishingly small, the term is probably not necessary.

 

Input is as follows:

 

GSAG SN GN NZONES [ X / Y ].

 

Here, SN is the surface assigned G-coefficients, GN is the coefficient you want to check, NZONES is the number of points to analyze (with a maximum of 50), and the X/Y choice determines the sample direction.

 

For example, here is a telescope with two aspheric mirrors.

 G 16  7.295454E-31(R**10)

Indeed.  The sag contribution from that term is 0.0151 mm at the edge, or about 48 fringes in double pass.  Looks like it's in there for a reason, so we leave it alone.

 

Note how the sample area is decentered from the vertex of the surface in the above example.  That is automatically done if the CAO is itself decentered, as it is in this case because surface 31 has been assigned the DCCR attribute.

 

This feature can be run on many kinds of non-spherical surfaces -- but be aware that the meaning of the G-terms varies and that not all are appropriate for this feature.  You are advised to only use it for terms that directly affect the surface sag.

 

GSAG is also an option in the MAP program.
