SYNOPSYS lens cell editor

 

This feature lets you design a lens cell and then prepare drawings of the individual parts.  A lengthy tutorial is found in Section 13.17.

 

Drag to define:

Click here, and then click-and-drag the mouse in the area above the lens elements where you want the top cross-section of a cell part to go.

 

Define from data

Enter data for the OD, ID, etc. into the edit boxes, and then click this button to define a new cell part with those parameters.

 

Tie->

Clicking here opens the Cell Editor Coordinate Transfer Dialog, where you can make parts contiguous or extend them to a lens surface at a particular place.

 

Drawing editor

This button opens the drawing editor, where you can define which parts of your lens cell you want to draw.

 

Edit this part

Here is where you can enter the part number of a cell that you want to edit.  You can also double-click the top cross-section of any cell part.

 

Click here and then just click in the top cross-section of a cell part to edit that part.

 

New (0,0)

Use this button to shift all cell parts to the left or right.  If you change the range of surfaces that are to be included in the cell, then the display in PAD will shift.  This feature lets you shift the cell so that it again lines up with the lens elements.

 

Use these buttons to assign a color to each part of your cell.  Parts that are of the same color and contiguous can be drawn as a single, complex, part by the drawing editor.

 

Save cell,

Open cell

Save and open cell designs with these buttons.  You must restore the PAD display the way it was when a cell was first created, if you want to open an old file.

 

Delete part

Deletes the current cell part.  It will not be shown, but it does not actually get deleted until the next Save or Checkpoint.

 

Delete entire

Click here to erase the entire cell design and start over.

 

Checkpoint,

Load check

These buttons save a snapshot of the current cell design, and can then restore it.  It works like an instant UNDO, but only to the last checkpoint.

 

Clearance

This value can be added to a lens diameter to ensure that your cell will clear the lens.

 

Micro

Clicking this will cause the sliders to move in finer increments.

 

Rays

This toggles the display of rays through the lens.

 

Numbers

This toggles the display of cell part numbers.  Note that these can change after a Checkpoint or Save, as deleted parts are eliminated.

 

Outlines

This toggles the filling of cell parts with color, or the display of their outlines only.

 

Close

This button closes the Cell Editor.  You will lose any cell data that you may have entered if you do not save them first or create a checkpoint.
